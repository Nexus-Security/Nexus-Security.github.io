Diversity, equity, and inclusion marketing ideas in June

While diversity, equity, and inclusion should be ever-present in your marketing, here are some June observances to spotlight in your content:

LGBTQ+ Pride Month

Pride Month celebrates the LGBTQ+ community’s spirit of freedom, creativity, and self-expression. Why not infuse your campaigns with these vibrant themes? Consider a pride art or pride flag contest! Encourage your social media followers, or even local businesses and non-profits, to design their own pride flags or art and share them on social media using a designated hashtag. This allows you to gather and showcase the entries before selecting the winners.

Image source Another idea, depending on your business, is to offer a couples-themed service, like a couples massage or photoshoot.

Black Music Appreciation Month

Black artists have profoundly shaped the music world. This month, elevate Black creators by crafting a shareable playlist featuring your favorite songs from Black artists. This unique approach helps you stand out while fitting diversity and inclusion into your marketing. It also boosts brand recall, as the playlist keeps your business top-of-mind for your audience. You could even host a listening event where you and your customers enjoy the playlist together.

Juneteenth (June 19)

Newly recognized as a federal holiday in 2021, Juneteenth carries over 155 years of history. Consider pausing regular operations and giving your employees the day off to reflect on and learn from Black history. Meanwhile, collaborate with a local Black-owned business or organization. Whether you’re volunteering at a Black history center or cross-promoting a Black-owned brand, share this partnership on social media to educate and inspire your customers.

Other June observances promoting diversity, equity, and inclusion are:

  • National Caribbean American Month
  • National Soul Food Month
  • PTSD Awareness Month
  • National Loving Day (celebrating interracial marriage) – annually on June 12
  • Anne [Frank] and Samantha [Smith] Day – bi-annually on the summer solstice

June marketing ideas for monthly themes

June marks National Safety Month, National Great Outdoors Month, and more. Let’s explore engaging ways to market your business in June these June awareness initiatives.

Safety Month

Safety is universally relevant. Here are some evergreen blog post ideas for different audiences:

  • Cyclers: 10 Essential Helmet Features for Your Child; 10 Fatal Mistakes to Avoid When Cycling at Night
  • Parents: Quiz: How Safe Is Your Baby Really?
  • Businesses: 5 Ways to Fortify Your Network Against Hackers

Great Outdoors Month

Time to swap lockdowns for the great outdoors! People are raring to get outside after extended indoor time. Here’s how to incorporate this theme:

  • Create a blog or social media post showcasing the best outdoor destinations in your state. Invite followers to share their favorite spots.
  • Run a month-long email campaign highlighting new outdoor ideas and destinations each week.
  • Take indoor classes outdoors!
  • Embrace the open air with a sidewalk sale.
  • Partner with a local outdoor gear retailer for product donations for an “outdoors essentials” kit or offer a discount to your customers. In return, promote their business on social media or reciprocate with product donations.
  • Keep it simple with inspiring outdoor imagery and captions.

Summer vacation/graduation

Celebrate the end of the school year with specials tailored to high school or college grads. Here are some ideas:

  • Moving companies can offer promotions for grads.
  • Restaurants can offer gift cards to grads who share a picture of themselves in their cap and gown at your establishment.
  • Catering and event businesses can highlight their remaining available dates.
  • Don’t forget graduation and teacher gift sales!

Marketing ideas for June national days

These unique days offer opportunities to capture attention and showcase your brand’s personality. Let’s explore!

Flip a Coin Day

June 1st is Flip a Coin Day, initially intended to celebrate math, but ripe with marketing potential. Consider these ideas:

  • Run a coin flip discount day: Customers flip a coin with each purchase, calling heads or tails. Winning customers receive a percentage discount based on the coin’s value: Quarter flips = 25% off, dime flips = 10% off, nickel flips = 5% off. Adjust discounts to your budget and customer volume.

  • Create content around decision-making. These blog post title templates work for various industries: Don’t Leave It to Chance: Making Informed Choices for [X] X or Y: Which Is Right for You? Confused About X? This Guide Can Help Don’t Flip a Coin When Choosing Your Next [industry] Partner.
  • Gyms and trainers can share or organize a coin flip workout.

Image source

Donut Day

The first Friday in June brings the irresistible combination of Fridays and donuts: National Donut Day!

  • Establish a new tradition: Local bakeries, go beyond discounts by announcing a limited-edition, never-before-seen donut for Donut Day. Tease it on social media and your website to build excitement. Continue this tradition annually for a highly anticipated event.
  • Fitness businesses can offer or promote an after-Donut Day workout. Donuts, while tasty, don’t exactly scream fitness. Help customers work off those delicious indulgences.
  • Show appreciation for your customers: Bakeries can post pictures of their loyal patrons (with permission), tagging them.

Or, simply indulge your audience with a visual treat by searching for “incredible donuts” on Google.

This one deserves to be called the Batman donut.

World Environment Day

Consumers gravitate towards businesses that champion their values, including environmental protection. Observed annually on June 5th, here’s how to demonstrate your support:

  • Organize charity donations or offer limited-edition eco-friendly products.
  • Provide waste-reducing freebies with purchases, like reusable bags or metal straws.
  • Even visually appealing Instagram posts on June 5th using earthy tones can make a statement.

Best Friend Day

Celebrated annually on June 8th, National Best Friend Day offers a relatable theme for sales and promotions. Run buy-one-get-one deals, promote your referral program, or host a tag-a-friend social media contest.

Iced Tea Day

Whether or not everyone’s a fan, National Iced Tea Day (June 10th) heralds the arrival of summer and sunshine, putting everyone in a good mood. Celebrate with these simple ideas:

  • Partner with a local coffee shop or bakery specializing in tea for a pop-up event at your business, offering free tea or coupons for free tea with purchase.
  • Boost foot traffic and brand awareness by distributing iced tea samples at your brick-and-mortar location.
  • Engage your social media audience with questions about their favorite iced tea flavors, share fun facts, or post refreshing iced tea recipes for increased engagement.

Image source

Nature Photography Day

Nature Photography Day, celebrated annually on June 14th, goes beyond simply capturing scenic shots. It’s about leveraging imagery to raise awareness and advocate for the conservation and protection of plants, wildlife, and landscapes. Share captivating nature photos on social media, accompanied by impactful statistics highlighting the need for conservation.

Summer solstice

The summer solstice, the year’s longest day, marks the official start of summer. It’s the perfect time to launch summer collections, host beach-themed events or sales, or offer beach blankets or koozies with purchases.

Image source

Take Your Dog to Work Day

This delightful day falls on the Friday after Father’s Day. Encourage employees to bring their furry friends to work and share the canine fun on Facebook. The promise of adorable pups is a great incentive for customers to brave the summer heat and visit your store.

And naturally, it’s a prime opportunity for a contest.

Final June marketing ideas

Here are a few bonus ideas to round things off:

  • Repeat Day (Saturday, June 3): Reintroduce successful past promotions and campaigns.
  • Cancer Survivor’s Day (Sunday, June 4): Showcase a team member or customer who’s a cancer survivor or share inspirational stories, quotes, and images.
  • Selfie day (Wednesday, June 21): Encourage your audience to strike a pose!
