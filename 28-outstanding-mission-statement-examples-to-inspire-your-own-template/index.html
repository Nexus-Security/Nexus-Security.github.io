28 Outstanding Mission Statement Examples to Inspire Your Own (+Template!)

Mar 09, 2024
16 minute read

All companies sell something. All companies are unique. And all companies want to present themselves in the best possible light, both physically and online. Of course, your social media posts, website content, paid ads, and emails should reflect that. However, it’s also crucial to have a central message that ties everything together.

That’s where your business mission statement comes in. This impactful statement captures the essence of your company and provides customers and employees with a clear understanding of what it stands for.

Despite being brief, condensing your business into a sentence or two is challenging. That’s what we aim to help you achieve. This article will guide you step-by-step on how to do just that.

What is a mission statement?

A strong business mission statement encapsulates your organization. It summarizes your company’s purpose and presents it to the public in an easily understandable way. This includes:

  • The products/services you offer
  • Your target audience
  • Your area of operation
  • Your operational methods
  • The ultimate value/positive impact of your products/services.

That last point is crucial. While your mission statement should be factual, it also needs to convey authenticity and inspiration. Let’s look at two examples to illustrate this:

An uninspiring mission statement example:

We construct Class A commercial office buildings for upscale clients in the Greater Boston area, employing our unique management system to deliver projects according to specifications, on time, and within budget.

While this statement includes all the necessary information, it lacks emotion.

An inspiring mission statement example:

In contrast, this mission statement from Davids Tea is full of engaging words and phrases that evoke feeling: We travel the globe searching for expertly crafted, single-origin loose leaf teas to share with our North American community. We’re constantly innovating and pushing boundaries with our tea blends. We want to make it effortless to experience, enjoy, and benefit from tea.

Mission statement vs vision statement vs values

Mission statements are often confused with other elements of a brand’s identity. Let’s clarify the distinctions:

  • Mission statement: This defines what you do, why you do it, how you do it, and the value you provide.
  • Vision statement: Your vision statement outlines what you aspire to become by fulfilling your mission.
  • Goals: Your goals are measurable outcomes that contribute to achieving your mission.
  • Core values: Your core values guide your team’s actions and underpin everything else. By pursuing your goals through the lens of your core values, you fulfill your mission, which in turn, fuels your vision.

Everything is interconnected. Here’s an illustration of a mission, vision, and core values:

  • Example mission statement: We empower people to save money while protecting the environment by combining innovative, sustainable packaging with wearable technology that educates, inspires, and promotes environmentally conscious behavior.
  • Example vision statement: To revolutionize how we approach environmental conservation.
  • Example values: Future-oriented, knowledge is power, assume positive intent, sustainability.

It’s important to note that not all businesses have separate vision and mission statements‚Äîthey are sometimes combined.

How long should a mission statement be?

A business mission statement should ideally be one to three sentences, approximately 100 words. It shouldn’t be an essay detailing the company’s origin story or the development of its vision. That information belongs on your “about us” page. Your mission statement should be concise, purposeful content that serves as a subheading for your company. Consider your mission statement a branding element‚Äîsummarize your company’s purpose in a few memorable sentences or less.

‚ÄúChargify integrates cutting-edge technology, subscription management expertise, and a customer-centric approach to solve real-world problems. We are dedicated to becoming the operating system for your subscription-based business.‚Äù

How to write a mission statement for business

Condensing your business into a few sentences is demanding, but our six-step process will help you write your company‚Äôs mission statement:

  1. Take stock
  2. Address the key questions
  3. Condense your ideas
  4. Infuse personality
  5. Share it with the world
  6. Adapt as necessary

1. Take stock

Begin broadly. Brainstorm with your team (or by yourself) and analyze the following:

  • What is your primary business type?
  • What are your concrete deliverables?
  • What makes your deliverables stand out?
  • What problems do your deliverables address?
  • What benefits do your deliverables offer?
  • What is your distinct value proposition?
  • What is your brand’s personality?
  • What is your unique approach?
  • What are your advantages over competitors?
  • What are your team members’ strengths and unique skills?
  • Who are your ideal customers?
  • What are your general audience segments or personas?
  • Do they reside in a specific location or region?

2. Answer the key questions freely

Compile your answers to these questions and use them to address the five key questions below:

  1. What you do
  2. How you do it
  3. Who you do it for
  4. Where you do it
  5. What ultimate benefit/value you deliver Write freely‚Äîthis is the creative part of the process. Note that sometimes, elements from the list are combined or omitted. There’s no right or wrong way to craft a mission statement.

3. Whittle it down

Now, distill each answer into a phrase or two for each of the elements mentioned above. You’ve probably identified words and phrases you resonate with, so this step should be straightforward.

Now, plug them into the following formula:

We [what you do] by [how you do it] for [target customers] to [ultimate value you provide].

You can rearrange the order as you see fit.

We [provide this value] to [target customers] by [what you do] through [how you do it].

Let’s consider AnyClip‚Äôs mission statement as an example: AnyClip’s mission is to enhance viewer engagement by leveraging its metadata expertise to provide a tailored viewer experience.

  • What they do: Deliver a personalized viewer experience.
  • How they do it: By utilizing their expertise in metadata.
  • Ultimate benefit: Increase viewer engagement.

4. Add color

While it’s essential to avoid overly descriptive adjectives and adverbs that hinder readability, consider if adding or substituting words can evoke emotion, like Campaign Monitor does with “deliver,” “complex,” and “beautifully uncomplicated.” ‚ÄúWe deliver technology that solves complex problems in a beautifully uncomplicated way.‚Äù

3. Make it public

Now it’s time to showcase your mission statement! Here are some effective ways to do so:

  • Dedicate a page on your website to your mission, vision, goals, and values.
  • If it’s concise enough, feature it as your homepage headline.
  • Include it on your Facebook or LinkedIn company page.
  • Link to it in your job postings.

4. Adjust as needed

Change is inevitable. New leadership emerges. Businesses rebrand or merge. Goals and strategies evolve. Culture transforms. Regularly review and update your mission statement annually to ensure it remains relevant.

Business mission statement tips

  • Use clear and simple language. Your employees and customers shouldn’t need a dictionary to understand your core values. Employ straightforward sentence structure and vocabulary that an eighth-grader can comprehend.
  • Highlight your unique value. This is your hook for potential customers. Communicate why you are the optimal choice‚Äîhow their business will benefit from yours, and vice versa. For instance, imagine you’re an international internet-based voice-call mobile app with the current mission statement: “Talk more.” But‚Ä¶why?! Rework it to include an inspirational or motivational element, like: “Talk more. Share stories, cultures, and perspectives from around the globe.”
  • Celebrate it: Ideally, your mission statement will become a guiding principle for your employees and customers. Acknowledge and reward behaviors aligned with your mission statement to reinforce your values and integrate them into your company culture.
  • Focus on the bigger picture. The most effective company mission statements encompass the overarching reason for the business’s existence and its contribution to a better world. Whether it’s improving access to information, promoting environmental sustainability, striving for equality, or combating poverty, the “bigger picture” can be a guiding force for growth and engagement.

Business mission statement templates

As mentioned earlier, here’s a basic mission statement template:

We [what you do] by [how you do it] for [target customers] to [ultimate value you provide].

You can also reorder the elements:

  • We [provide this value] to [target customers] by [what you do] through [how you do it].
  • By [how you do it], we [help target customers] [achieve ultimate value] through [product/service]. For a more inspirational tone, consider this template:

We help/empower/equip [target customer] to achieve/reach/eliminate [desire/painpoint] by providing/building/creating [tangible deliverables].
