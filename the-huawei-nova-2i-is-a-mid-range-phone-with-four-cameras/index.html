The Huawei nova 2i is a mid-range phone with four cameras

May 01, 2024
2 minute read

Source: Huawei website. The nova 2i boasts a 5.9" FullView display.

Huawei has released the nova 2i, a 4G LTE mid-range phone designed for users who love taking selfies. This device stands out with its four cameras - two in the front and two on the back.

Clement Wong, Director of Global Product Marketing for the Huawei Consumer Business Group, highlighted that four cameras are uncommon in mid-range phones. He explained that Huawei aims to make its cutting-edge technology accessible to a wider customer base. The company’s focus, he added, is on optimizing battery life and camera capabilities to deliver an exceptional user experience.

Equipped with a 16nm octa-core Kirin 659 chipset, the phone combines performance with style. Its “nearly borderless” 5.9" FHD bezel-less display is housed within a body typically used for 5.5" screens. The dual front cameras (13 megapixels with f/2.0 aperture and 2 megapixels) and dual rear cameras (16 megapixels and 2 megapixels) provide greater photographic flexibility.

The portrait mode generates natural bokeh effects, allowing users to simply tap on the screen to select their focus point and blur the background. A “live” photo function for both selfies and rear camera shots enhances images with a 2-second video clip. Huawei’s camera technology also enables “refocusing” - adjusting the focal point after an image is captured to achieve different depth-of-field outcomes.

Sophisticated algorithms ensure high-quality selfies even in low-light conditions. There’s a selfie toning flash with adjustable light intensity for balanced, soft lighting, and Snapchat-like overlays for adding a creative touch to images.

Interested?

The nova 2i is currently available in the Philippines, Singapore, and Malaysia, offered in Aurora Blue, Graphite Black, and Prestige Gold. It features 4GB of RAM, 64GB of ROM (expandable up to 128GB with a microSD card), and runs on the Android 7-based Emotion UI (EMUI) 5.1 operating system.
