118 Innovative Blog Post Ideas for Businesses (Including Examples and Templates!)

Oct 21, 2023
15 minute read

Fact: When businesses prioritize consistent blogging, they experience a significant surge in traffic and leads, surpassing those that don’t by 3.5x and 4.5x respectively. Fair point: This holds true, provided that blogging remains a regular practice. Fiction: Maintaining a consistent blogging schedule is a walk in the park.

The advantages of frequent blogging often take time to manifest, typically months. While these benefits have a compounding effect, their realization hinges on your ability to sustain the strategy, which can be challenging. If your blog feels like a garden parched by drought, take heart! You’re not alone, and a downpour of inspiration is just around the corner. Continue reading for 118 blog post ideas to invigorate your content strategy and reignite your passion, complete with illustrative examples and Google Doc templates!

Establishing Your Expertise

The goal here is to position yourself as a thought leader, differentiating your business within your niche and establishing authority.

  • X Things [Hobby] Can Teach You About [Topic]: What passions lie beyond your business? Can you forge a connection between them and concepts relevant to your audience? Thought leadership isn’t always about pioneering new ideas; it can also involve presenting existing ones in fresh and engaging ways.
  • Tapping into Expert Opinions: Is there a topic within your niche that sparks diverse viewpoints, a topic that invites a range of perspectives without being emotionally charged? Pose the same question (or set of questions) to several experts, gaining a comprehensive understanding. For instance, explore our Ultimate Guide to PPC Metrics, where 17 experts share their insights on the top 3 must-check PPC metrics.
  • X Best Practices I Don’t Follow: Are there industry standards that you find counterproductive, outdated, or simply ineffective in your context? Share them along with your reasoning. However, to avoid misguidance, be specific about your rationale and illustrate situations where those best practices actually prove their worth.
  • Crafting Authentic Lists: Every niche has those overused keywords that yield endless search results, all echoing the same information. In our field, it’s Instagram captions—a source of frustration, to say the least. Break free from the mold and become the exception—the one result that provides genuinely original content while still aligning with the keyword’s intent. Highlight this distinction in your SEO title. In fact, that’s precisely what I did in my post: [131 [NOT Overused!] New Year’s Instagram Captions.]
  • X Things I Wish I Knew / Mistakes I Made: Sharing your missteps can be just as impactful as showcasing your achievements. It humanizes you. Reflect on mistakes you’ve encountered in your professional journey or insights you wish you had gained earlier. Your readers will appreciate the transparency, and even more so, the opportunity to avoid similar pitfalls.
  • Sharing Your Perspective: Express your viewpoint on an industry topic. However, ensure you present opposing opinions or alternative perspectives. Our post on Performance Max exemplifies this approach, exploring its pros, cons, and the polarized views surrounding it.

Dive deeper into this concept.

Expert Insight:

By nature, thought leadership content might not attract substantial organic traffic initially, as you’re introducing novel ideas your audience might not be actively searching for. However, you can leverage social sharing, email marketing, and internal linking to amplify its reach. Authentic thought leadership often earns valuable backlinks as well.

Expanding Your Thought Leadership Repertoire

  • Unveiling Unexpected Connections: What Does [Topic A] Have to Do With [Seemingly Unrelated Topic B]?
  • Shifting Perspectives: A [Role]’s Perspective on [Something Outside of That Role]
  • Sharing Invaluable Advice: The Best Piece of [Topic] Advice I’ve Ever Received
  • Unveiling Your Journey: Share the story behind your business (valuable content for your About Us page as well)
  • Distilling Knowledge: Share your top takeaways from a recent presentation
  • Book Reviews: Review a book within your niche, be it a new release or a timeless classic.
  • Engaging with Influencers: Interview an influencer or expert in your field.

Strategy & tip blog post ideas

This category forms the core of your content strategy. Some businesses hesitate to share their methods, fearing they’ll reveal trade secrets. But you don’t have to divulge your most guarded tactics. By demonstrating how to accomplish something, you establish your expertise and genuine desire to help. If they can’t find it on your blog, they’ll find it elsewhere, and if they can DIY, they weren’t your ideal client anyway. Target those who lack the skills, time, or desire to learn what you do.

  • Achieving X Without Y: Explore achieving a desired outcome without resorting to conventional strategies or tools. For instance, we have a post on “How to Compete With Big Spenders (Without Spending More Money).”
  • The Power of Time-Bound Solutions: People crave efficiency and predictability. Offering solutions within a specific timeframe—a week, an hour, five minutes—especially if it’s faster than what they’re accustomed to, is a surefire way to pique their interest.
  • Our Journey to X: Share your path to achieving a specific goal (one your customers also aspire to), detailing the strategies employed, mistakes made, and challenges overcome, to guide others on their own journeys. Naturally, avoid revealing your secret sauce.
  • Topic: The X-Step Recipe to Success: Transform a process into an analogy, guiding your readers through it step by step, just like a recipe. Include ingredients (necessary tools, skills, mindset), steps, images, and a generous sprinkle of food puns. Here’s a delicious example: How to Run Google Ads: The Step-by-Step Recipe to Success.
  • The Quickstart Guide to X: Just like a product often comes with comprehensive instructions and a concise quickstart guide, condense one of your more detailed walkthroughs or whitepapers into a post covering the essentials. You can also call it a cheatsheet or pocket guide.

Delve into this insightful example.

A Word of Caution for Bloggers:

When targeting a keyword with any of these ideas, ensure your content aligns with the keyword’s intent to improve its chances of ranking. Sometimes, SEO and creativity require careful balancing.

Repurposing blog post ideas

Repurposing content is a win-win situation, maximizing your efforts and keeping your content fresh with minor updates, all while saving you valuable time.

  • Distilling Wisdom: Your Top Tips Compiled. Scour your existing posts on a particular topic, extracting the most valuable, niche-specific tips your audience might not stumble upon easily. Combine them into a single post—a curated collection of actionable hacks.
  • A Collection of Winning Strategies. Similar to the previous idea, instead of extracting specific tips, gather posts that delve into different strategies in depth. Create a post offering a brief overview of each strategy, linking to the original detailed posts for further exploration.
  • Consolidating for Impact. Do you have numerous short posts covering the same keyword cluster, potentially competing with each other on search engine results pages? Consider merging them into one comprehensive post and redirecting accordingly. Choose the URL with the most traffic or backlinks, or if none stand out, publish as new and redirect all to the new link.
  • Transforming Conversations into Content. Have any social media posts sparked lively discussions or Q&A threads? Repurpose them into blog posts!

Company culture blog post ideas

This category often intersects with thought leadership. These posts inform, inspire, entertain, and express. And don’t forget to promote them on LinkedIn!

  • Employee Roundup: If you have a sizable team, create a quick, engaging survey or poll, focusing on topics like stress management, your core values, or a current (non-controversial) event. Share the results in a fun, visually appealing write-up, including quotes from open-ended responses (with permission, of course).
  • Core Values Series: Dedicate a monthly post to each of your core values. Dive deep, providing examples, relevant quotes from famous figures, employees, even customers, and internal photos. You could even feature a different business each month that embodies those values or, if you’re B2B, highlight a client’s business.
  • Job Opening Roundup: Instead of simply posting openings on job boards and social media, get creative with a blog post. Use engaging descriptions, visuals, and even exclusive information not found elsewhere. You might attract a higher caliber of applicants this way. For example: Our Marketing Team is Growing (& How You Can Help!).

Product support blog post ideas

While these posts have a promotional aspect, you can make them brand-agnostic to broaden their appeal and avoid sounding overly salesy. Let’s see how.

  • X Signs It’s Time for [Product or Service]: Instead of dictating needs, present relatable signs and symptoms your audience can identify with. Subtly weave in how your product or service addresses these pain points, planting a seed for future consideration.
  • Choosing Wisely: What to Look for in a [Your Service] Provider: This builds trust. Guide readers towards an informed decision, even if it means choosing a competitor. This “But You Are Free” technique, acknowledging their freedom to choose, often sways them in your favor. See our post “10 Questions to Ask When Choosing a PPC Agency” for inspiration.
  • Addressing Your Audience’s Questions: Latest FAQs. If dedicated community management or a knowledge base isn’t feasible yet, but product-related questions are common, create a series answering frequently asked questions received recently.
  • Case Studies: Proof in Action. A cornerstone of marketing, case studies also make for compelling blog content! Publish them as standalone posts or adapt existing ones into blog-friendly narratives for a more engaging read. Our case study tips can help you with this.

Data-driven blog post ideas

Data-driven posts attract valuable backlinks, boosting your SEO. Proprietary data is ideal but often scarce. Here’s a mix of ideas to get you started.

  • Industry Benchmark Data: Do you have customer data you can share? Our advertising benchmarks reports, for instance, are always popular. People are curious to see how they measure up against industry peers.
  • State of the X: If your customer base is large enough, consider surveys or polls to gather insights. Both customers and non-customers find value in comparing themselves to the overall results. For smaller customer bases, poll your social media audience or, budget permitting, expand to non-followers. Our “State of the Agency” series is a good example.
  • Mega Stats Lists: Compile the latest stats on a niche topic, diligently crediting sources.

Explore an illustrative example.

A Crucial Data Point:

People love data, but not everyone enjoys deciphering numbers. Use visuals generously. Create branded images (Canva makes it easy), linking to the source and including their name directly on the image, especially if adapting their visual. Don’t be afraid to innovate. If there are more effective ways to present data than the commonly replicated graphs, be the one to do it.

Expanding Your Data-Driven Content

  • Curated Stats List: Extract key stats from your mega list and elaborate on them, providing actionable takeaways.
  • Benchmark Comparison: Gather and compare benchmarks from various online reports.
  • Data-Backed Strategies: X Data-Backed Strategies/Reasons to
  • Highlighting Importance: X Stats That Highlight the Importance of [Topic]
  • Debunking Myths: X [Topic] Myths Busted (With Data!)
  • Leveraging Trends: Use Google Trends data
  • Data-Driven Storytelling: Share a success story/case study with data

Event, seasonal & holiday blog post ideas

These are the posts your audience anticipates eagerly.

  • Monthly Idea Series: What creative inspiration do your customers crave each month? Team-building activities? Social media strategies? Family fun? Create a post for every month and update it annually with fresh ideas. Our monthly marketing ideas posts are a testament to this approach.
  • Gift Guides: Suggest gift ideas relevant to your niche and audience. Focus solely on your products or create a diverse list that includes yours.
  • Themed Challenges: Announce a challenge you’re undertaking—individually, as a team, or involve your audience—and report on it at the end of the month/season. Share your experiences: the highs, the lows, the lessons learned.
  • Event Predictions: Is there a major annual event in your industry? We have Google Marketing Live, and we’ve shared our predictions for its content in the past. A little self-congratulatory post when your predictions hit the mark never hurts.
  • Conference Takeaways: Distilling a conference or event into key takeaways is highly appreciated by those who couldn’t attend or want to compare notes.
  • National Day/Month Inspiration: Which national days resonate with your business or values? Be it World Kindness Day, National Children’s Dental Health Day, or even National Backward Day, the possibilities are endless. Here’s an example: August is National Black Business Month. Here’s How to Get Involved.

News-related blog post ideas
