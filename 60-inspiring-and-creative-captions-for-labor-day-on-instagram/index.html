Unique Labor Day Instagram Captions

If you’ve seen my other Instagram caption posts, you know I’m not a fan of the overused ones flooding the internet. I prefer taking those and adding an authentic spin. Here are some examples:

Instead of “Celebrate work by not doing any.”

  • Celebrate work by working harder. (Share a picture of yourself fully immersed in a fun project).
  • Celebrate work by working hard to celebrate.
  • Celebrate the hustle by not moving a muscle.
  • #celebratelaborday with some much-needed R&R What are your plans for the long weekend? Share in the comments below - we’ll feature the best ones on our story

Instead of “Work hard. Party harder.”

  • Work hard. Party harder. Clean-up hardest. (Or switch it up with “Clean-up easy” if you’re in the cleaning business.)
  • Work hard. Play harder. Party hardest.
  • Work hard, try hard, fail hard, win hard.

Save full-size image

Instead of “Happiness is not having to set your alarm for Monday.”

  • Happiness is eagerly setting your alarm every day because you genuinely love your job.
  • Happiness is earning a living doing what you’re passionate about. This is a great opportunity to express gratitude to your customers for making your work so enjoyable.
  • Happiness is working with an amazing team you can’t wait to see after a long weekend.
  • Happiness multiplies when shared! Here’s how we celebrated Labor Day with our favorite people this year

Instead of “The only thing better than a long weekend is the short work week that follows it.”

  • The only thing better than a long weekend is indulging in some delicious short [ribs/bread/cake]! Imagine you’re a restaurant, enticing your audience with a mouthwatering photo of short ribs, bread, or cake. Get creative and think outside the box!
  • The only thing better than a long weekend is sipping on a refreshing Long Island iced tea during a long weekend
  • The only thing better than a long weekend is _______. Let us know in the comments!

Instead of “Never stop chasing summer”

  • Dear summer, ever considered staying…like, forever?
  • Summer, wait! I promise to pack light.
  • RIP Summer! You will be missed
  • Farewell, Summer! Welcome, Fall

Labor Day Facts

Share some insightful Labor Day facts and statistics from the History Channel to educate your audience and raise awareness:

  • Labor Day originated from the labor movement in the late 19th century and became a federal holiday in 1894.
  • In the late 1800s, the typical American endured 12-hour workdays and 7-day workweeks.
  • Labor Day was officially declared a law on June 28, 1894, by President Grover Cleveland.
  • Peter J. McGuire, co-founder of the American Federation of Labor, and Matthew Maguire, secretary of the Central Labor Union, are widely recognized as the key figures behind the establishment of Labor Day.
