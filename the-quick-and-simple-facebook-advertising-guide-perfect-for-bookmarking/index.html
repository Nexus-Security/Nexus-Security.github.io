The Quick and Simple Facebook Advertising Guide (Perfect for Bookmarking!)

Sep 30, 2023
7 minute read

Facebook advertising offers a budget-friendly way to boost your brand’s visibility in today’s market. They’re not only easy on the wallet, but they also offer massive reach. However, a surprising number of small businesses aren’t maximizing their Facebook ad campaigns. A study by Weebly revealed that 62% of small business owners find their paid Facebook ads miss the mark. It’s concerning considering the platform’s widespread use among small businesses. Despite this, small business owners can leverage these ads to connect with a global audience of billions. More crucially, they can reach specific demographics most likely to convert into customers. While getting the hang of it initially might seem daunting, the payoff is substantial.

This concise guide offers a simplified understanding of Facebook advertising. It covers fundamental Facebook advertising principles, including:

  • Facebook ad formats
  • Facebook ad expenses
  • Facebook ad audience targeting
  • Facebook ad content and visuals
  • Maintaining a competitive edge on Facebook
  • Evaluating your Facebook ad performance

Different Types of Facebook Ads

When starting with Facebook ads, it’s crucial to understand your tools. Let’s begin with the various ad types at your disposal. Facebook provides a range of ad types, including:

  • Image – Straightforward yet visually appealing ads using high-quality images (more details here)
  • Video – Ads incorporating sound and motion (learn more about Facebook video ads here)
  • Carousel – Showcase up to 10 images or videos within a single ad, each with its unique link (more on Carousel ads here)
  • Slideshow – Ads that weave sound, text, and motion to narrate brand stories
  • Collection – Ads that highlight products from your shop’s inventory
  • Instant Experience (formerly Canvas) – Immersive, full-screen experiences optimized for mobile, accessible directly from ads (more on Canvas ads here)

  • Lead Generation – Image, video, or carousel ads that, upon engagement, present leads with a form (more on Lead Ads here)
  • Offers – Ads promoting discounts
  • Post Engagement – Ads aimed at amplifying page posts for increased interaction
  • Event Responses – Image or video ads designed to raise awareness and gather responses for events
  • Page Likes – Ads intended to boost page likes and encourage engagement The ideal ad type for you hinges on your business model and marketing objectives, so establish those first. Free guide » 7 Essential Facebook Advertising Tips for every advertiser.

Expenses Associated with Facebook Ads

Facebook remains immensely popular among businesses despite controversies surrounding data handling. Similar to Google Ads, a bidding system determines ad placement and cost-per-click. You specify your budget for actions like clicks, conversions, views, etc. You can either manage this manually or let Facebook optimize it based on your criteria. Several factors influence your Facebook ad costs, such as:

  • Campaign timing (time of day, day of the week, peak hours with heightened competition)
  • Target audience
  • Relevance Score
  • Ad placement
  • Bidding strategy (setting bid limits per bid or an average bid) As you can imagine, ad costs can fluctuate significantly. However, averages offer some insight. nexus-security analyzed numerous client accounts to determine the average cost-per-click (CPC) and cost-per-action (CPA) across 18 industries on Facebook. They found overall averages of $1.72 per click and $18.68 per conversion.

Remember, these are averages. Your actual costs may differ depending on your campaigns.

What if you’re outside the US? AdEspresso conducted a study analyzing 2017 data to compare average ad costs in the US and other regions. Here are the findings in US dollars:

  • Average CPC across all countries – $0.97
  • Average CPC targeting individuals aged 65+ – over (Q1) $0.70
  • Average CPC targeting the 13-17 age group (Q1) = $0.11
  • Average CPC for Instagram ad placement (Q4) – $1.15
  • Average CPC for Facebook ad placement (Q4) – $0.50
  • Monthly average CPC – $0.40
  • Average CPC on Sundays – $0.40
  • Average CPC on Tuesdays and Thursdays – $0.50
  • Average CPC targeting women (Q4) – $0.64
  • Average CPC targeting men (Q4) – $0.50
  • Average CPC when optimizing for link clicks (Q4) – $0.44
  • Average CPC when optimizing for impressions (Q4) – $3.79
  • Average CPL (cost per like) for page like campaigns – $1.08
  • Average CPL targeting individuals aged 55+ (Q4) – $0.33
  • Average CPL targeting the 13-17 age group (Q4) – $0.04
  • Monthly average CPL – $0.12
  • Weekly average CPL (Q4) – $0.14
  • Average CPL for targeting women – $0.16
  • Average CPL for targeting men – $0.11 Understanding ad costs helps estimate how much of your budget Facebook marketing will consume. You can always fine-tune your targeting and other strategies to minimize these costs.

Targeting Your Audience with Facebook Ads

Let’s dive into ad targeting—identifying your audience and tailoring ad content that resonates with them. Your ability to pinpoint your audience significantly impacts ad spending, ROI, and overall effectiveness. Investing extra time in defining your target audience is worthwhile.

Facebook allows for granular audience definition. Its extensive targeting options include (but aren’t limited to):

  • Gender
  • Age
  • Location
  • Language
  • Interests
  • Behaviors
  • Life events (e.g., “recently married”)
  • Education
  • Job title
  • Income
  • Political affiliation These demographic parameters help refine who sees your ads. For instance, if you’re targeting teens on the west coast who are multilingual and interested in entertainment products, your ad targeting might look like this:
  • Male/female
  • 13 to 19
  • Washington/Oregon/California
  • English/Spanish
  • Entertainment > Live events > concerts Focusing on those most likely interested in your offerings yields better results than broadly targeting everyone.

Crafting Compelling Facebook Ad Copy and Creative

Like any advertising, your Facebook ad copy and creative heavily influence ROI.

Mastering persuasion is crucial in advertising. Psychologist Robert Cialdini’s 1984 book, Influence: The Psychology of Persuasion, outlined six principles to enhance marketing persuasion and conversion:

  • Reciprocation
  • Social proof
  • Commitment and consistency
  • Scarcity
  • Authority
  • Liking Incorporating these timeless principles into your Facebook ads is recommended. Consider how to captivate your audience and entice clicks using elements like:
  • Headline (keep it concise, clear, and memorable)
  • Placement (learn more about optimizing Facebook ad placements here)
  • Images (opt for vibrant, attention-grabbing visuals)
  • Value proposition (what sets you apart; your unique offering)
  • Call to action or CTA (use a clear, action-oriented verb to drive action) How can you apply these principles? Reciprocation could involve offering a free report or ebook for completing a lead form. Social proof could be as simple as showcasing the number of reactions, shares, and comments your ad receives. More creative, engaging ads tend to garner more likes and shares. Find more tips on writing effective Facebook ads here.

Staying Ahead of the Competition on Facebook

Observing and learning from your competitors is fair game. Delivering superior content to your customers attracts their attention and loyalty. Your competitors’ Facebook ads are a treasure trove of inspiration. Analyze their strengths and weaknesses. Implement these insights in your ads, especially when vying for the same audience.

For instance, both Skillshare and Udemy are online learning platforms offering courses and tutorials.

While Udemy’s ad might promote lifetime access to a single course with a money-back guarantee, Skillshare’s might highlight unlimited access to all courses for just $0.99 for two months. Examining competitor ads can spark ideas for new value propositions, CTAs, or emotional angles to test in your Facebook ads. Discover more strategies for competitive advertising on Facebook here.

Reporting and Analyzing Your Facebook Ad Performance

Don’t expect to launch campaigns and passively await results. Actively manage them to ensure a positive ROI on your ad spend and efforts. Start monitoring results immediately after launching a campaign. Don’t wait until its conclusion to assess its effectiveness. Use Facebook Ads Manager to track real-time performance (or try our free Facebook ads grader!). Real-time monitoring allows for immediate adjustments to underperforming ads. Easily edit your ads within the Ads Manager to optimize their impact.

For example:

  • If engagement is low, refine the ad copy or visuals to make them more compelling.
  • If reach is lower than anticipated, consider expanding your target audience.
  • If conversions are lagging, improve your call to action with a more action-oriented verb or enhance your Facebook ad landing pages. Finally, prioritize tracking meaningful metrics like sales and ROI or ROAS (return on ad spend) over vanity metrics like engagement. A campaign with high reach, clicks, and conversions is ineffective if it results in a net loss per sale after expenses.

Therefore, prioritize tracking ad performance and sales throughout your campaigns.
