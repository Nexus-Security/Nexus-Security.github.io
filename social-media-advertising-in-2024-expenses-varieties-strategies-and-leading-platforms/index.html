Social Media Advertising in 2024: Expenses, Varieties, Strategies, and Leading Platforms

Nov 07, 2023
15 minute read

Digital marketing relies heavily on social media, but unlike other channels such as search engines and email, its strength lies in the sheer number of platforms it encompasses. This isn’t a simple “Bing versus Google” scenario; each platform boasts a vast and distinct audience, ready for targeted advertising.

Simply put, social media advertising is a force to be reckoned with. However, when effectively managed, it can propel your business beyond its objectives. This comprehensive guide will equip you with everything you need to know to launch and thrive in the world of social media advertising.

What is social media advertising?

Social media advertising, a subset of social media marketing, leverages paid media to boost your business’s presence across various social channels. This media comes in diverse formats and placements, accommodating a wide spectrum of creative content, from images and videos to immersive experiences.

Often referred to as “paid social,” it differs from paid search, which focuses on keywords. Paid social hones in on audiences, their interests, and online behavior. Social media ads tend to be more visual, brand-focused, and seamlessly integrated into the platform, sometimes making them difficult to distinguish from regular posts.

Benefits of social media advertising

Similar to any Pay-Per-Click (PPC) strategy, social media advertising offers numerous advantages for businesses. Let’s explore some compelling reasons to incorporate it into your marketing mix:

  • Cost-effectiveness: PPC, in general, delivers an impressive average ROI of 200%. Paid social, specifically, presents a more budget-friendly approach compared to targeting your competitors’ audience through search engine advertising.
  • Brand awareness: Social media networks offer immense reach, and visually appealing ads, tailored to your brand’s identity and style, amplify your visibility.
  • Engagement: Social media ads go beyond simple clicks. Users can actively engage by liking, sharing, and commenting, fostering a sense of community around your brand.
  • Formats: Social media ads embrace versatility, supporting a diverse array of formats and media types to effectively convey your message.
  • Targeting: Thanks to the detailed information users provide on these platforms, social media ads allow for highly precise targeting, ensuring your message reaches the right eyes.
  • Non-disruptive: Social media ads often blend seamlessly with organic content, providing a less intrusive way to connect with your target audience.

Social media advertising stats

How do social media ads work?

While search ads predominantly rely on keyword targeting, social ads revolve around audience targeting. Each platform boasts unique features, settings, and formats, yet they share these common threads:

  • Auction style: Mirroring Google Ads, social ads operate on an auction-based PPC model. An algorithm determines ad placement and cost based on various factors, including ad quality, maximum bid, and historical performance.
  • Campaign objectives: Most platforms require you to first pinpoint your campaign objective. Options typically represent the top, middle, and bottom of the sales funnel:
    • Reach/awareness
    • Leads/engagement
    • Conversion/sales
  • Targeting: Platforms empower you to target users based on demographics, interests, and behavior. You can also leverage retargeting and create lookalike audiences. Each platform has nuances, but this guide to Facebook audience targeting offers a valuable overview.
  • Account structure: While terminology might differ, each platform provides an account structure to organize ads into groups, which are then grouped into campaigns.

How to get started with social media advertising

Taking that first step often poses the biggest hurdle in any marketing endeavor. However, by tackling these fundamental steps, you’ll gain the confidence to dive in:

1. Understand your audience

Like any successful marketing strategy, a deep understanding of your target audience is paramount. Leverage your existing customer data, conduct thorough market research, and analyze data from your current platforms to gain insights into what resonates. This helps you pinpoint who to target, the most effective messaging, and the optimal timing for your ads. As you gather data from your campaigns, you can refine your targeting even further.

2. Choose one channel to start

While a multi-platform strategy is crucial, mastering one platform at a time is key. The ideal starting point depends on your audience, but Facebook often proves to be a solid choice for beginners.

3. Establish some general goals

Define what you aim to achieve with your social media ads. Don’t get bogged down in setting overly specific SMART goals just yet—it’s difficult to determine concrete metrics before launching your campaigns. Focus on identifying a general campaign objective based on your primary goal, be it raising awareness, generating leads, driving conversions, or something else entirely.

4. Look at benchmarks

While your social media ad costs depend on factors like your industry, audience, optimization strategy, and more, understanding industry benchmarks provides valuable context for budget planning.

Social media advertising tips

While the most effective strategies vary by platform, these universal tips pave the way for success:

Lean on your organic data

If you’re considering social media advertising, chances are you’re already engaging in organic social media marketing. Use your organic performance data to inform your paid strategy. Over time, ad data will provide ample insights, but initially, analyze audience demographics, high-performing posts, underperforming posts, and engagement metrics. This reveals your most receptive audience segments and the offers that resonate most. Utilize native analytics within each platform or any social media reporting tool to unlock these valuable insights.

Use user-generated content

In addition to your most engaging posts, incorporate high-performing user-generated content as ad creative. These authentic testimonials, blending relatability with reviews, make for compelling, ROI-driven, and genuinely impressive ads.

Keep mobile in mind

This may seem obvious, but its importance cannot be overstated: prioritize the mobile experience. Most social media platforms are primarily accessed on mobile devices; some are even mobile-exclusive. While adhering to image specifications naturally optimizes your ads for mobile, consider your audience’s mobile usage when crafting offers and scheduling ad delivery. For instance, if you’re targeting young, urban professionals, promoting a lengthy PDF guide on LinkedIn during their morning commute might not be ideal. It might be more effective to run the ad when they’re likely on their desktops.

A/B test

Continuously experiment with different ad variations to determine what resonates best. A/B testing involves changing one element at a time—be it the copy, the image, or, as demonstrated below, the image style.

If there was an Airbnb for cats…

Use multiple campaign types

Remember, each platform offers various campaign objectives—not just to cater to diverse business goals (like app installs versus store visits) but also to address the different stages within your sales funnel. Consider generating leads with a top-of-funnel offer in one campaign while retargeting website visitors with a more bottom-funnel offer in another. Create multiple campaigns and leverage the available objectives to construct a comprehensive full-funnel strategy, maximizing your return on each platform.

Pair with search

Once you’ve established a solid social media advertising strategy, explore its synergy with your search advertising efforts. For example, incorporate high-performing keywords from your Google Ads data into your Facebook ad copy or target a specific Facebook audience based on their interaction with a search ad offer.

Don’t let your creative get stale

Social media feeds are highly visual and constantly refreshed, making it crucial to keep your ad creative fresh and engaging.

YouTube advertising

Snapshot

YouTube boasts the most extensive advertising reach among all social media platforms, with an audience of 2.56 billion users. This translates to a staggering 32.4% of the global population, with the 25-34 age group reigning supreme. The average cost per click for YouTube ads is $3.21, and the average CPM is $9.68.

Types

YouTube provides six primary ad formats:

  • Skippable in-stream ads
  • Non-skippable in-stream ads
  • Bumper ads
  • Outstream ads
  • Masthead ads
  • Overlay ads

Pros and cons

Video ads are renowned for their engagement and effectiveness. However, capturing users’ attention on platforms where scrolling and muted videos prevail can be challenging. YouTube, being video-centric, offers a distinct advantage in this realm. It’s important to acknowledge that video ads can sometimes be disruptive and require significant resources to create. Nevertheless, users spend a significant amount of time on YouTube (on par with TikTok) averaging 45 minutes daily, providing ample opportunities to connect with your target audience.

LinkedIn

Performance snapshot

LinkedIn has an advertising audience of 808.4 million, representing 10.3% of the global population. The platform’s dominant demographic is 25-34 years old, with an average cost per click of $5.26 and an average CPM of $6.59.

Types

LinkedIn presents four core ad types:

  • Sponsored Content
  • Sponsored Messaging (Sponsored InMail)
  • Text Ads
  • Dynamic ads

Don’t be fooled by this seemingly limited selection. Formats include carousel, conversation, follower, spotlight, job, lead generation forms, message, single image, single job, text, and video. That’s an extensive array of ad types!

Pros & cons

LinkedIn has a reputation for being a pricey advertising platform (its average CPC is four to five times higher than Facebook’s ). Many advertisers also point to the platform’s lack of features found elsewhere, such as advanced rule-setting and delivery optimization for specific actions.

Despite these drawbacks, LinkedIn offers powerful audience insights and targeting capabilities.
