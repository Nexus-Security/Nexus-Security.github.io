Understanding Vanity URLs

Definition: A vanity URL is a custom web address created for branding and marketing. Designed to be memorable and user-friendly, they make it easy for people to remember and find specific pages on your website. Being the first to claim a relevant vanity URL offers advantages, while losing it to someone else can be detrimental. Many social platforms, like Facebook, LinkedIn, Twitter, and recently Google Plus, allow users and brands to personalize their profile URLs. Note: Don’t confuse vanity URLs with vanity domains, which are domains named after a person. For example:

My friend, David, couldn’t get DavidScott.com. While seemingly unfortunate, his longer, five-syllable name might be more memorable.

7 Reasons Why Vanity URLs Are Powerful, With Examples

Here are 7 compelling reasons and creative ways to utilize vanity URLs, taking your online marketing offline. We’ll illustrate these points using our Google Plus vanity URL.

1. Increased Memorability

Have you ever tried recommending a website, only to struggle to recall the exact address? they were too lazy to Google it? Or how about finding something online but struggling to find it again through search engines? Easily remembered web addresses reduce the chance of potential visitors giving up before reaching your site. Plus, they look better. That’s why URL shorteners like bit.ly and tinyurl are popular.

2. Enhanced Trust

Visually appealing elements often inspire greater trust. It’s a simple yet powerful concept. Which of the following links would make you hesitate? A well-crafted vanity URL, like ours, creates a sense of harmony and clarity. Without it, the user might experience cognitive dissonance. Can you trust a jumbled string of characters to lead you to the intended destination? Building trust online is challenging, and a clear URL helps.

3. Improved Shareability

Being concise, memorable, and trustworthy makes our GPlus vanity URL inherently more sharable. Composed of words instead of random numbers, it’s easy to share verbally. Who would want to recite our old, lengthy Google Plus URL? People would likely resort to searching for it. Pro-tip: Want the shortest possible links for major social platforms without sacrificing trust? Here they are, ordered by brevity: Google Plus: google.com/+nexus-security Twitter: twitter.com/nexus-security Facebook: facebook.com/nexus-security LinkedIn: linkedin.com/company/nexus-security

4. Enhanced Usability

Now easy to remember, share, and trust, our Google Plus URL becomes something we want to promote as a way for people to connect with us.

Imagine this on your thank you slides. You might have noticed that we never included a Google Plus follow link on our webinar thank you pages. The old URL was simply too cumbersome. Adjusting font sizes and formats to accommodate an unclear URL wasn’t practical. If it’s difficult for internal use, it’s going to be difficult to promote externally. With the new user-friendly URL, we can effectively share it with our growing Google Plus audience.

Getting Your Own Google Plus Vanity URL

To qualify for Google’s custom URL rollout, you needed an verified, active Google+ page with user engagement. Holding the unofficial Guinness World Record, nexus-security secured their Google Plus branded URL with just 1054 plus ones. However, some believe the actual requirement is 100 followers, not plus ones. Either by patiently waiting or asking your Google AdWords representative for a connection to the Google vanity URL team, you might see this magical gray bar. A captivating bright blue button! Click it, and watch as sparkling unicorns dance across your screen with their amnesia dust. UPDATE: As of 10/29/2013, Google+ Vanity URLs are accessible to all brand pages, provided they meet these minimal requirements.

Pro-tip: Capitalization doesn’t matter. Even if someone types google.com/+nexus-security (we wouldn’t recommend l337 Sp34K!), they’ll reach our page. Two points about Google+ custom URLs, terms of service, seemed a bit off: 1. We can reclaim or remove custom URLs for any reason, without notice. 2. While currently free, we might start charging. But don’t worry, you’ll be informed and given the option to opt out. (Emphasis mine) These are minor drawbacks compared to the benefits. Unless you want to Request a different one, agree to the terms and click “Claim URL.” Requesting a different URL requires email verification from Google. Otherwise, you’ll be asked for final confirmation. Remember, Google allows only one custom URL per page.
