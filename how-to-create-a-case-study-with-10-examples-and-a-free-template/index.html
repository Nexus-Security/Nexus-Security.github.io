How to Create a Case Study (with 10 Examples and a Free Template!)

Jul 08, 2024
11 minute read

Case studies are a cornerstone of marketing. They can be incredibly powerful for attracting customers but are often tedious and dull. The issue? A bored reader won’t become a customer. Let’s change that! This guide reveals the secrets to crafting case studies that captivate your audience. We’ll explore how to make them captivating, motivating, and persuasive, transforming readers into loyal clients.

What is a case study?

A case study is essentially a compelling narrative showcasing how a real customer conquered their challenges using your products or services. Notice the emphasis on “story.” While marketers frequently talk about “storytelling,” a successful case study embodies this concept. It presents a protagonist (your customer) facing a problem, ultimately achieving victory.

Intercom’s case study effectively personalizes its protagonist, Atlassian. A well-crafted case study allows readers to envision themselves as the heroes of their own narratives. Why write a business case study?

Simply put, case studies are incredibly effective for converting leads into customers. Here’s why:

  • Foster customer loyalty: It’s an opportunity to engage your satisfied clients and reinforce their reasons for choosing you, encouraging continued loyalty.
  • Empower sales: Beyond website publication, salespeople can leverage case studies in discussions with potential customers, instilling confidence.
  • Content versatility: Quotes and data from case studies can enrich your homepage, product pages, landing pages, and more. Repurpose them into PDFs, videos, blog posts, and infographics.
  • Build trust: Case studies transform positive customer feedback into concrete evidence of your value. It’s among the most trusted content types according to 60% of marketers.

How long should a case study be?

A compelling case study can be concise and impactful, even fitting within a single page. Of course, length can vary depending on the industry. For instance, a kitchen remodeling business might rely heavily on visuals, while software solutions require more detailed explanations. Here’s a general guide:

  • Aim for 500-1500 words.
  • Include a prominent snapshot section (100 words or less).
  • Devote the majority of the word count to results and benefits.
  • Prioritize clarity and conciseness. Let your data, visuals, and customer testimonials speak volumes.

What a marketing case study is NOT

A case study should be an objective, data-driven resource that reinforces your brand, building trust with potential customers. Here’s what it shouldn’t be:

  • Press releases: While case studies can complement product launches, they’re not merely platforms for promoting new offerings. Strive for evergreen case studies for maximum impact.
  • Advertisements: While elements of case studies can enhance landing pages or ad copy, the case study itself shouldn’t be an advertisement. Focus on presenting factual information, not hype.
  • Company-centric: The customer’s journey should take center stage. Many case studies fall short because they prioritize brand voice and messaging over the compelling power of storytelling. Avoid excessive focus on your company’s achievements.

How to write a case study: steps & format

Now that we understand the essence of a marketing case study, let’s break down the writing process. A compelling case study includes:

  1. Impactful headline: Similar to a news headline, it should convey the most crucial information. You can add a subtitle with supporting details or a customer quote.
  2. Concise snapshot: Provide a brief overview at the beginning, including the client’s name/industry, the product/service used, and key result statistics.
  3. Client introduction: A concise introduction (one or two sentences) describing the customer and their notable attributes.
  4. The challenge: Clearly articulate the problem or goal the customer faced, the potential consequences, and any hesitations they had. Incorporate quotes for authenticity.
  5. The solution: Explain how they discovered your business, their reasons for choosing you, the chosen solution, and its implementation process. Include quotes to personalize the experience.
  6. Tangible results: Highlight the outcomes and advantages, including any unexpected benefits. Support your claims with quotes, statistics, before-and-after visuals, or other compelling evidence.
  7. Conclusion: Conclude with further customer praise, advice for similar businesses, and a call to action.

Tips on how to write a case study that prospects will want to read

Here are eight tips to elevate your case studies from informative to captivating, turning them into powerful sales tools.

1. Simplify the process for the client

Requesting a case study should be as seamless as asking for reviews. When approaching clients, clearly communicate your intention to feature their success story. Provide clear details, including:

  • The process: (A 20-minute interview, followed by a draft for approval)
  • Case study placement: (Your website, PDFs for sales reps, etc.)
  • Interview options: (In-person, phone/video call, email)
  • Benefits for the client: (Social media exposure, for example) Transparency fosters willingness to participate.

2. Include a prominent results snapshot

While storytelling is key, don’t keep readers in suspense. Present the results upfront, enticing them to discover the journey. The example below clearly communicates the key takeaway with the title (The Loot Box Uses Ad Factory and Content Marketing to Drive Sales) and the supporting statistics.

3. Find an interesting angle

Making case studies compelling can be challenging. Infuse creativity:

  • Feature unique customers with unconventional product usage or extraordinary situations.
  • Connect your industry with theirs through a thematic approach (consider puns).
  • Start with a teaser highlighting the most compelling result.
  • Incorporate the client’s personality to make it relatable. A captivating angle enhances engagement. In Mailchimpâ€™s case study example below, the customer name (Good Dye Young), a strong headline, and a powerful image create a captivating effect.

6. Position yourself as the supporting character

Present your company as a guide, helping the true heroâ€”your clientâ€”overcome obstacles. This approach is effective for two reasons: It allows readers to envision themselves as the protagonist and enhances your credibility through humility.   • Staples replatformed in half the time
  • Bombas saved $108,000 a year

7. Let your clients tell their story

Craft a narrative that gives your client a voice. Incorporate their direct quotes for authenticity. Use an interview style, paraphrasing, and annotations to avoid repetition. Set the stage and allow your client’s words to shine.

8. Set realistic expectations

While case studies are valuable resources, they might not garner the same level of traffic or engagement as other content. Their primary function is to assist prospects already considering your offerings. Don’t be discouraged by potentially lower traffic; focus on quality and relevance to your target audience.
