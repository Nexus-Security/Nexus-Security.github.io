The top 10 workplaces in Singapore have been revealed

Jul 15, 2022
12 minute read

A recent study by the research and consulting firm Great Place to Work Institute Singapore reveals a strong connection between high levels of trust and engagement among employees and a high-performing work environment.

In addition to the new report, Singapore Insights 2015, Great Place to Work Institute Singapore has unveiled its first-ever 10 Best Companies to Work For in Singapore List. Salesforce, the cloud computing giant, claims the top spot. Many companies on the list are, unsurprisingly, tech-focused: Cisco, InterContinental Hotels Group, Singaporean development consultancy ROHEI, and SAP Asia round out the top five.

‚ÄúRelationships are highly valued in Singaporean companies," says Joni Ong, Managing Director at Great Place to Work Singapore. “We have seen that the best companies possess a clear vision for their culture and have implemented HR practices designed to bring that vision to life. These practices are put into action by leaders and managers with authenticity and a human touch, resulting in positive everyday experiences for employees. This is what separates the best from the rest.‚Äù

Great Place to Work has been studying exceptional workplace cultures and publishing best workplace rankings globally for over three decades. Their consistent finding is that trust, pride, and a sense of community are the universal cornerstones of a great workplace. The process for selecting Singapore‚Äôs Best Companies to Work For mirrors that of the Fortune 100 Best Companies to Work For.

Two-thirds of a company’s score is determined by the results of the Trust Index Employee Survey, which is distributed to a randomly selected group of employees from each company (ensuring a 95% confidence level). The remaining third is based on responses to the Culture Audit. This audit delves into detailed questions about compensation and benefits packages and includes open-ended questions regarding recruitment practices, internal communication methods, training programs, recognition initiatives, and diversity efforts.

The list acknowledges that exceptional workplaces in Singapore share a common thread: a robust corporate culture defined by high levels of trust, camaraderie, and pride. Employees at these top 10 companies also place a premium on effective management and well-structured workplace policies and practices.

The Singapore Insights 2015 report delves deeper into these findings, illustrating how outstanding cultures translate into a competitive edge. It provides insights into how companies can foster employee loyalty and enhance their perception as a great place to work.

The complete list of Singapore‚Äôs Best for 2015 is also included in the report. These Best Workplaces represent six diverse sectors, spanning information technology, hospitality, manufacturing, production, and professional services.
