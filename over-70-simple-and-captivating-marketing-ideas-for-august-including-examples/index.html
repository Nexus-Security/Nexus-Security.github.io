Diversity, equity, and inclusion marketing ideas in August

Integrating diversity, equity, and inclusion into your marketing strategy should be a continuous effort for any thriving business. Here are a few August-specific ideas:

Celebrating Black-Owned Businesses Throughout August

Black Business Month, established in 2004 by John William Templeton and Frederick E. Jordan, presents a prime opportunity to not only support Black-owned businesses but to make it a consistent practice.

  • Retailers: Embrace the 15% Pledge by incorporating products from Black-owned businesses into your inventory. Utilize the Support Black-Owned Businesses Directory to discover such businesses in your vicinity.
  • B2B Companies: Consider interviewing a Black business owner and sharing their insights on entrepreneurship, navigating prejudice, or addressing common challenges faced by your clients.
  • All Businesses: Educate your followers on practical ways to support Black-owned businesses.
  • Black-owned Businesses: Leverage Google’s “Black-owned” attribute tag for enhanced visibility.
Related: Our friends at LocaliQ share 13 more ways you can support Black Business Month!

Womenâ€™s Equality Day: Commemorating Progress and Advocating for More (Aug 26)

Observed annually on August 26th, Womenâ€™s Equality Day commemorates the day women gained the right to vote in the US back in 1920. However, the journey toward equality continues, with women still striving to overcome various obstacles. Here’s how your business can actively participate in honoring this significant day:

  • Showcase the Accomplished Women in Your Business: Shine a spotlight on the incredible women who contribute to your business’s success. Share their stories and highlight their invaluable contributions. This not only strengthens your employer branding but also demonstrates to your customers that you genuinely value the women within your organization.
  • Bridging the Gender Pay Gap: Despite progress, women, on average, still earn 82 cents to every $1 than their male counterparts. While efforts to close this gap continue, consider offering your female customers an exclusive discount as a gesture of support and to raise awareness about gender pay equality. An 18% discount on your products or services can make a tangible difference.

August marketing ideas based on monthly awareness

As evident from the diverse range of observances, August offers ample opportunities to connect with your audience on an emotional level. Leverage these ideas to foster deeper, more meaningful connections than your competitors.

Family Fun Month: Making the Most of Summer’s End

  • Collaborate with local attractions: Partner with nearby amusement parks, zoos, museums, or entertainment centers to offer exclusive coupons or discounts to your customers.
  • Curate a Family-Friendly Guide: Create a blog post or an email newsletter featuring a roundup of your favorite family-friendly destinations and activities in your city or town.
  • Target Parents with Relatable Content: If your target audience consists primarily of parents, craft a blog post or social media update that addresses the challenges of the last days of summer. Acknowledge the potential for sibling rivalry and parental burnout after weeks of limited alone time. Offer practical tips and relatable humor to create a sense of camaraderie and provide genuine value.
  • Organize a Family Fun Night: Collaborate with other local businesses to host a family-oriented event. Photographers can offer family portrait sessions, restaurants can provide catering and coupons, and businesses like preschools or childcare centers can set up informative yet engaging booths.
  • Run Themed Sales: If you’re in the apparel industry, consider running a series of weekly sales targeting different family members â€“ men, women, babies, and teens â€“ throughout August.

Back to School Season: A Second Chance for New Beginnings

Back-to-school season shares a similar vibe with January, offering a fresh start and a chance to gear up for new challenges. Repurpose those goal-setting and fresh-start themes from your January marketing campaigns for a second wave of engagement.

  • Back-to-School Specials: Salons can offer discounted haircuts, spas can entice parents with relaxing massages, and schools and after-school programs can incentivize early registration with attractive deals.
  • Promote Wellness and Fitness: Personal trainers and fitness centers can offer back-to-school fitness packages, while dentists and nutritionists can encourage checkups and provide healthy lifestyle tips.
  • Re-engage Dormant Customers: Launch a re-engagement campaign to reconnect with customers who went quiet during the summer months. Offer exclusive discounts or promotions to reignite their interest.

Simplify Your Life Week: Embracing Minimalism (First Week of August)

In today’s fast-paced world, “seeking simpler living” has emerged as a significant consumer trend, according to Amazon. As a business owner, use this week to declutter both your physical and virtual spaces, organizing your workspace and your mind before the fall season kicks in. Here are some marketing ideas to align with the theme:

  • Free Trials and Discounts: Productivity tool providers can offer free trials or discounted subscriptions during Simplify Your Life Week.
  • Share Your Simplification Strategies: Create content sharing the tools, apps, or methods you use (or have abandoned) to simplify your life.
  • Expert Organization Tips: Interior designers or professional organizers can offer valuable tips and tricks for decluttering and organizing homes or workspaces.
  • Demystify Complex Topics: Break down a complicated topic related to your industry into easily digestible language for your audience. August marketing ideas based on national days

This month presents a prime opportunity to unleash your creativity with unique August social media posts and marketing campaigns.

National Friendship Day: Celebrating Bonds and Referrals (First Sunday of August)

This special day provides the perfect occasion to promote referral programs and launch enticing “buy one, get one” sales promotions. Consider using catchy slogans like “You never know how many friends you have until you own [your product]” to encourage referrals and engagement.

National Dollar Day: Making Cents of Savings (Aug 8)

On August 8, 1786, the United States established its monetary system â€“ a historical fact that doubles as a fantastic marketing opportunity.

  • Financial Literacy Content: Publish a blog post or social media update with money-saving tips, educating your audience on how to save a dollar (or even hundreds).
  • Dollar-Off Deals: Offer a $1 discount on your lower-priced products to drive sales and create a sense of urgency.
  • Special $1 Offers: Create an irresistible offer by featuring a specific product or service for just $1, enticing customers to try your offerings.

National Book Lovers Day: Connecting Through Literature (Aug 9)

Every audience includes bookworms. Celebrate this day by sharing a social media post recommending your favorite reads, highlighting influential authors in your industry, or inviting followers to share their own recommendations. This fosters engagement and provides valuable insights into your audience’s interests.

National Relaxation Day: Prioritizing Well-being (Aug 15)

August is National Wellness Month, and what better way to promote well-being than by dedicating a day to relaxation?

  • Spa and Salon Promotions: Spas and salons can offer exclusive discounts or even organize short, complimentary chair massages at local offices to promote their services.
  • Signature Drink Specials: Restaurants and bars can create special promotions around signature cocktails or mocktails, positioning them as the perfect way to unwind after a long day.
  • Free Yoga Sessions: Yoga instructors can offer free introductory sessions at local businesses or community centers, promoting the benefits of yoga for stress reduction.
  • Encourage Employee Well-being: Encourage your own employees to take a well-deserved break by offering an early dismissal or a relaxed dress code.

National Spirit of â€™45 Day: Honoring Resilience and Unity (Second Sunday in August)

On August 14, 1945, President Truman announced the end of World War II, marking a pivotal moment in history. The unwavering national pride, the indomitable “can-do” spirit, and the collective willingness to step up and contribute not only secured victory but also laid the foundation for future generations. The Spirit of ‘45 serves as a powerful platform for crafting inspiring blog posts, email campaigns, and social media updates that resonate with your audience.

World Humanitarian Day: Inspiring Action and Compassion (Aug 19)

World Humanitarian Day honors those who have selflessly dedicated their lives to humanitarian causes, often making the ultimate sacrifice. Here are some meaningful ways to incorporate this day into your marketing:

  • Support a Worthy Cause: Choose a local humanitarian organization and donate a portion of your proceeds from a specific day or product to their cause.
  • Encourage Acts of Kindness: Inspire your audience to make a difference by suggesting simple acts of kindness they can perform in their communities.
  • Share Stories of Modern-Day Heroes: Feature quotes and stories of contemporary humanitarians like Eddie Aijuka to shed light on current global challenges and the individuals working tirelessly to address them.

Senior Citizens Day: Showing Appreciation for Our Elders (Aug 21)

While senior citizens often enjoy year-round discounts, why not go the extra mile on their designated day?

  • Senior Living Community Events: Host an open house barbecue or a special event to honor your residents and provide an opportunity for potential residents (and their families) to experience your community firsthand.
  • Local Business Outreach: Connect with local senior centers to explore sponsorship opportunities, cater an event, or even offer complimentary services like manicures to brighten the residents’ day.

Secondhand Wardrobe Day: Promoting Sustainability and Giving Back (Aug 25)

Numerous local thrift stores operate as part of non-profit organizations, supporting crucial needs both locally and globally. Consider organizing a clothing drive to empower your audience to make a difference while decluttering their wardrobes â€“ perhaps even aligning it with the Simplify Your Life Week theme. National WebMistress Day: Celebrating Women in Tech (Aug 26)

This day recognizes the invaluable contributions of women in web development. Show your support by acknowledging #womenwhocode, featuring a female developer from your company in an employee spotlight, or making a donation to organizations like GirlCode that support aspiring female coders.

National Just Because Day: Embracing Spontaneity (Aug 27)

This whimsical day provides endless opportunities for creative marketing.

  • Surprise and Delight Your Subscribers: Send your email subscribers an unexpected promo code simply to show your appreciation for their support.
  • Encourage Impulsive Acts of Joy: Encourage your customers to indulge in a little spontaneity by purchasing that coveted item, booking that impulsive trip, or simply doing something out of the ordinary â€“ just because.
  • Break the Rules of Traditional Marketing: Challenge conventional copywriting wisdom and promote a product or service with a simple “just because” approach, emphasizing the joy of treating oneself.
  • Inspire Random Acts of Kindness: Encourage your audience to spread positivity by performing a random act of kindness â€“ no reason required, just because.
  • Run a “Just Because” Giveaway: Boost engagement and spark joy by hosting a giveaway with no strings attached.
  • Create a “Just Because” Bucket List: Craft a blog post or social media update suggesting a list of inspiring or whimsical things to do “just because,” encouraging your audience to embrace the spirit of the day. Even more August marketing ideas

You don’t need a specific August social media holiday or monthly event to fuel your marketing efforts this August. Here are some additional ideas:

Plan Ahead for State and County Fairs

Many state fairs occur in the fall, so start planning your involvement early.

  • Secure a Web Sponsorship: Increase brand visibility and gain valuable backlinks by becoming a web sponsor, showcasing your logo on the fair’s website.
  • Become a Vendor: Explore opportunities to participate as a vendor, offering food and beverages, renting out equipment like tables and chairs, or even providing entertainment options like bouncy houses.
  • Support Through Donations: Donate an item for an auction or raffle to demonstrate your community support and gain exposure for your business.

Embrace the Summer-to-Fall Transition

August’s weather can be unpredictable, with some regions experiencing lingering summer heat while others welcome cooler temperatures. Tailor your August summer marketing promotions to reflect these contrasting experiences. Whether you’re offering tips for savoring the final days of summer or building excitement for autumnal activities, aligning your marketing with the current climate is a surefire way to resonate with your audience.
