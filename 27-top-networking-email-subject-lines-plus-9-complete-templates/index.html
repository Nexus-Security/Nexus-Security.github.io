Mastering the Art of Writing Networking Emails

Networking involves a certain finesse, with individuals adopting unique approaches influenced by their profession, personality, and work style. However, certain fundamental principles apply universally to all networking emails.

Providing Value

Think of networking emails like a shared meal. Instead of overwhelming recipients with a large feast of information or a crowded platter of links and advice, offer a small, valuable side dish. This could be a single link to a relevant article, a helpful tool or resource, contact information for someone who could assist them, or even an offer to share your insights on a specific topic when you meet.

Brevity is Key

Craft your networking emails with conciseness in mind. Even if you established a strong personal connection and offered numerous tips and resources, avoid overloading the email. It’s best to establish the connection first and then share additional resources later or during a follow-up meeting.

Specificity and Personalization

Make your networking emails stand out by incorporating specific details. Mention the event where you met, propose specific dates and times for a follow-up meeting, and most importantly, reference a specific detail from your conversation to demonstrate attentiveness and make your email memorable.

Timeliness Matters

Following up promptly with new contacts from networking events is crucial. While immediate contact isn’t necessary, reaching out within a day while the event is still fresh in their mind is ideal.

Infuse Your Personality

While professionalism is essential, don’t be afraid to let your personality shine through in your networking emails and subject lines. Just as a brand distinguishes a business, your personality sets you apart from the crowd.

Adopt a Long-Term Perspective

Networking is a marathon, not a sprint. While prompt follow-up is important, don’t be discouraged by a lack of immediate response. Circumstances vary, and people may be busy. Remember that many contacts may not lead to regular interaction, but your thoughtful initial email will pave the way for future reconnections as needed.

Crafting Compelling Networking Email Subject Lines

The principles for crafting effective networking email subject lines generally align with those for general email subject lines, with the advantage that they are often easier to formulate.

Here are the key guidelines:

Keep it Concise: Aim for brevity, ideally 50 characters or fewer, allowing recipients to quickly grasp the email’s purpose.

Personalize: Tailor each subject line, even for similar emails sent after a networking event, by including the recipient’s name or a specific detail from your conversation.

Clarity is Key: Clearly convey the email’s purpose, especially if it includes a request. Directness is appreciated.

Now that we’ve covered the fundamentals of networking emails and subject lines, let’s explore some practical templates.

Follow-Up Networking Emails and Subject Lines

Following up within a day of connecting with someone at a conference, networking event, or social gathering is essential to solidify the connection. This demonstrates your interest and increases the likelihood of establishing a meaningful relationship.

Example #3: Follow-Up Email After No Response

Send this email as a reply to your previous email to maintain the same subject line and include the original message for context.

Template

Subject: Re: [original email subject line]

Hi [name],

Just wanted to follow up and see if you had a chance to review my previous email.

Please let me know if you’re available for a quick call on [propose a specific day] to discuss [briefly mention the topic].

Best regards,

[Your name]

Additional Subject Lines for Networking Follow-Up Emails

Here are some alternative subject lines for your networking follow-up emails:

  • Following Up on Our Chat at [Event/Place]
  • Great Meeting You Last Night!
  • Continuing the Conversation from [Event Name]/About [Topic]
  • Sharing the [Resource/Information] We Discussed

Reconnection Networking Emails and Subject Lines

Networking is an ongoing process, and reconnecting with past contacts is equally important as establishing new connections. Properly nurturing your network ensures that you have valuable connections to reach out to as your career or business evolves.

Example #4: Reconnection Email to Maintain the Relationship

This type of email is suitable for individuals you’ve interacted with multiple times, such as former colleagues or clients.

Template

Subject line: Checking In and Saying Hello

Hi [name],

Hope you’re doing well! I was recently [mention something relevant to your connection that made you think of them] and your name came to mind.

Just wanted to check in and see how things are going. I noticed [mention something specific about their work or company] - very impressive!

Wishing you all the best!

Best regards,

[Your first name]

“Cold” Outreach/Introductory Networking Emails and Subject Lines

First impressions matter, especially when sending unsolicited networking emails to individuals you haven’t met. These emails represent your personal and professional brand, so crafting a compelling message is crucial.

Unlike shorter networking emails, cold outreach introductions can be slightly longer to demonstrate your research and the value you offer.

Example #6: Informational Interview Request for Career Growth

This email template is suitable for reaching out to someone in a role or industry you’re interested in, a company with admirable strategies, or someone in a similar role to exchange insights.

While offering something specific in return might not be possible at this stage, a general gesture of reciprocity can be effective.

Using a direct subject line is recommended to avoid being mistaken for a sales or recruitment email.

Template

Subject line: Seeking Insights on Your Experience in [Industry/Role]

Hi [name],

My name is [your name] and I’m currently a [your current role] at [hyperlinked company name], a [brief company description]. I’ve been researching [mention the industry/role/company] and I’m incredibly impressed by your work, especially [mention a specific project or accomplishment].

I’d be grateful for the opportunity to learn from your expertise. Would you be available for a brief informational interview over the phone or video call sometime in the coming weeks? I’m eager to hear about your journey and insights on [mention specific areas of interest].

Thank you for your time and consideration.

Best regards,

[Your first name]

[Your full name] [Your company if applicable] [Your phone number] [Your website if applicable] [LinkedIn profile link]
