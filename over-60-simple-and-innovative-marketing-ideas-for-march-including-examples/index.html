March marketing ideas for DEI

Now, let’s delve into some specific ideas for the events and causes mentioned earlier.

Women‚Äôs History Month

Dedicate some time this March to recognizing the influential women in your business or field. For instance:

  • Launch an Instagram or Facebook giveaway encouraging participants to share stories of inspiring women from history and their impact in one line.
  • Showcase renowned women who have left their mark within your geographical area and industry.
  • Organize a webinar or event led by women, exclusively for women, or both!

International Women‚Äôs Day

International Women‚Äôs Day celebrates the achievements of women, raises awareness against bias, and encourages action towards equality. Each year centers around a different theme, which you can explore at internationalwomensday.com.

  • Share a brief history lesson on a female or group of women in your field or niche.
  • Ask your followers to share stories of influential women in their lives.
  • Use the year‚Äôs designated hashtag in your posts,
  • Introduce your customers to the women behind the scenes at your business.

Equal Pay Day

Commemorating the 50th anniversary of the Equal Pay Act, Equal Pay Day, typically observed on the second Tuesday in March, sheds light on the gender wage gap. Its scope has broadened in recent years to encompass women of color, LGBTQIA individuals, mothers, part-time workers, seasonal workers, and more. Consider these actions:

  • Support the Equal Pay Today campaign by contributing a portion of your sales.
  • Share impactful gender wage gap stats on social media.
  • Engage with women in your community through interviews or polls. This could involve your social media followers, customer base, professional network, or even your team.

Monthly awareness March marketing ideas

Even if your business doesn’t directly align with a specific March theme, a touch of creativity can help you create engaging and relevant content for any audience. Let’s explore some possibilities:

National Reading Month

If content reigns supreme, then reading is the ultimate power move. Here are some ideas:

  • Start a conversation by sharing your favorite books and inviting your followers to do the same.
  • Seek book recommendations from your audience.
  • Post thought-provoking quotes about reading or excerpts from your cherished reads.
  • Make a difference by organizing a book drive.

Caffeine Awareness Month

This month presents a treasure trove of opportunities to engage your audience across various social media platforms. Here are a few ideas:

  • Develop a captivating blog series, social media posts, or videos around a caffeine-related theme. Think eye-opening statistics, tips to boost savings, energizing content, or anything related to “roasting,” “ramping up,” or “activating.”
  • Challenge your audience (or yourself!) to a month without coffee. Offer helpful tips on achieving the effects of caffeine naturally. Document your journey and share the experience.
  • Host weekly “coffee chat” sessions on Facebook Live.
  • Don’t limit yourself to coffee‚Äîexplore other caffeine sources like tea, chocolate, soda, and energy drinks.
  • Simplify complex industry concepts using relatable coffee analogies (e.g., dark, medium, and light roasts).
  • When in doubt, tap into the endless supply of relatable coffee memes.

Credit Education Month

While ideal for businesses in finance, this month offers opportunities for all. Here’s how:

  • Host a webinar featuring a guest speaker who can provide valuable insights on improving credit scores. This topic resonates with anyone who makes payments for your products or services.
  • Show appreciation to loyal customers with a playful email. Instead of credit tips, offer them a $X credit on their account (March email subject line ideas here!).
  • Combine celebrations by linking credit education with women’s history.

National Nutrition Month

This is a great time for restaurants, health food suppliers, and fitness centers to market their products and offerings. You can:

  • Provide nutrition tips and healthy recipes through blog posts or email newsletters.
  • Run a special deal on your healthiest products.
  • You can also be a bit tongue-in-cheek and describe your business as ‚Äúfood for the soul‚Äù or highlight true health benefits your services provide that nourish your body or mind.
  • Share the latest research and facts and use hashtag #eatright and #NNM to get your posts noticed and shared.

March Madness

The NCAA men‚Äôs college basketball tournament tips off in March. There are plenty of ways to get into the spirit:

  • Create a March Madness bracket to go with your business.
  • Run a competition of your most popular dishes/products and encourage customers to order one of the two dueling items.
  • Small businesses in or around college towns can offer patrons a discount for wearing team colors.
  • Take pictures of your employees who are getting in the spirit and post them on social media with the hashtag for the team you support.

And even if you have nothing to do with sports, you can still take part. If a bridal boutique, an event planning company, and a dog treat business can find a way to jump on the bandwagon, anyone can.

First Day of Spring (March 19)

For those of us in seasonal climates, the first day of spring is always an exciting time, so take advantage of it with ideas like these:

  • Get people in the spirit with pastel-rich images on Instagram.
  • Run a sidewalk sale, regardless of the weather. If it‚Äôs chilly, you can offer hot tea and coffee. If it‚Äôs raining, get a tent. Make it a fun ‚Äúdetermined to stay positive‚Äù event.
  • Start a spring series on YouTube or refresh your YouTube channel trailer with a spring theme.

Employee Appreciation Day (first Friday)

A little recognition can go a long way in maintaining your employees‚Äô morale and productivity. Plus, high-spirited and engaged employees make for happier customers. Here are some ways to celebrate:

  • Introduce your team members in an Instagram Story or carousel post, with a little something about what each one brings to the table.
  • Ask your employees some fun questions and put a write-up of their answers into a blog post.
  • For B2B marketing, remind your customers to express their appreciation for their employees.

Keep in mind also that these types of gestures make your business more appealing to potential customers because they humanize your brand.

The first Friday of March is National Day of Unplugging and the perfect day for marketing your spa, yoga studio, or fitness center. Encourage people to visit your facility‚Äîoffer a special deal for that day‚Äîand give people a chance to disconnect from the busy world of the internet and smartphones.

Pi Day (March 14)

Pi Day is March 14 (3.14), not to be confused with actual Pie Day on December first of every year. Ideas for this mathematically magnificent day:

  • Schools, daycares, and after school programs can teach children about math and host fun Pi Day events.
  • Bakeries and restaurants, why not add an ‚Äúe‚Äù onto the end anyway and run specials on pies or pie baking classes.
  • Pi is used for measuring circles. Artists and designers can promote circular or geometric art.
  • Find different ways to use the numbers in pi. For example, run a sale of three days, 14 products, 15% off.

St. Patrick‚Äôs Day (March 17)

The luck of the Irish is here on March 17 for St. Patrick‚Äôs Day.

  • Get involved in your community by participating in St. Patrick‚Äôs Day parades or Shamrock Shuffle fun runs that are going on. Make sure to use the event‚Äôs hashtag to get your posts found by new followers and promote your business.
  • Have a green clothing contest in your office and share it on social media.
  • Give customers wearing green a special discount or prize for taking a picture and tagging your business.
  • Get festive and invite your followers to drop in for limited-time offerings inspired by the luck of the Irish.
  • Run a contest and invite people to test their luck! To get more Instagram followers, have them tag someone else in a comment on your post.
  • Write a thought leadership piece about luck.
