13-Step Guide to Creating a Brand Community (with Real-World Examples)

Feb 26, 2023
8 minute read

Want to increase customer loyalty, reduce your marketing budget, and get fresh ideas directly from your customers? A brand community might be the answer.

Take Sephora, for instance. Their Beauty Insider Community, launched in 2017, boasts over 5 million members and has generated more than 2.7 million user-generated posts. This exclusive platform offers makeup and skincare enthusiasts a space to connect, find inspiration, and get genuine product recommendations.

Creating a brand community allows your business to cultivate engagement, provide support, and encourage word-of-mouth marketing, ultimately driving growth. This guide provides a comprehensive overview of building a successful brand community, including inspiring examples.

Brand communities aren’t merely a marketing tactic; they represent a strategic approach aligned with your business objectives, facilitating growth in various ways.

Here are some additional advantages of fostering a brand community.

Enhanced customer loyalty and sales

A sense of belonging and connection with a brand often translates to greater loyalty.

Source When customers feel like they’re part of a community, they are more inclined to support and advocate for the brand. Moreover, loyal community members tend to make repeat purchases and play a vital role in attracting new customers through referrals.

Fostering genuine engagement

Brand communities provide a platform for organic interactions among customers, leading to authentic insights and deepening the emotional connection between the brand and its audience.

Establishing a valuable feedback loop

Brand communities offer businesses direct access to customer thoughts and feelings. This feedback is invaluable for product enhancement, innovation, and understanding market needs.

Source

Assistance with crisis management

A strong brand community can act as a safety net during challenging times. The company can communicate directly with its most loyal customers, fostering understanding, gathering feedback, and navigating difficult situations together.

Reduced marketing costs

Word-of-mouth marketing and organic sharing within the community can significantly reduce reliance on traditional marketing expenditures. Community members transform into brand ambassadors, often promoting products and services without direct incentives. Furthermore, 28% of customers prefer discovering new brands and products through word-of-mouth recommendations.

Source

A breeding ground for new ideas and improvements

Engaged community members often contribute fresh ideas, perspectives, and solutions, leading to product or service enhancements or entirely new offerings.

Building a thriving brand community

Building a brand community extends beyond simply setting up a platform; it involves nurturing relationships, providing value, and consistently engaging with your audience. A successful brand community requires strategic planning, continuous improvement, and a customer-centric approach.

1. Align with your strategy

Ensure your brand community aligns with your business objectives and customer satisfaction goals.

Instead of being an afterthought or a separate initiative, a successful brand community needs a clear purpose. Whether it‚Äôs strengthening brand loyalty, gathering user feedback, or driving organic word-of-mouth marketing, the community should reflect the brand‚Äôs core values and mission.

2. Allocate sufficient resources

4. Utilize your website

Hosting your community on your website simplifies first-party data collection, ensures consistent branding, and potentially increases website traffic and time spent on site. This integration can provide valuable customer insights and improve conversion rates.

Lululemon’s brand community, hosted on their website, is a great example.

5. Create a seamless cross-channel experience

A brand community shouldn‚Äôt be confined to a single platform. If your community thrives on your website, share snippets of engaging discussions, testimonials, or user-generated content on social media to create interconnected touchpoints that reinforce brand messaging and values.

6. Prioritize a customer-centric approach

Members are the heart of any brand community. Their needs, desires, and feedback should be paramount. A successful community provides a platform for users to express themselves openly and feel valued. Offering exclusive content, offers, or information can make community members feel like VIPs, strengthening their bond with the brand.

7. Act on customer feedback

Open communication channels will lead to an influx of customer feedback. What truly matters is how the brand responds and adapts. Demonstrating that you value and act on user feedback can turn casual users into passionate brand advocates.

8. Encourage active participation

Thriving communities require engaged participants. Involve users in product development or other brand initiatives. This approach helps source innovative ideas and gives users a stake in your brand‚Äôs journey, deepening their emotional connection.

10. Engage consistently

Engagement involves more than just content updates. Host live Q&A sessions, feature user-generated content, or spotlight community members. Offering tangible benefits, such as discounts or early access to sales, can also encourage participation.

Don‚Äôt underestimate the power of real-world interactions. Workshops, meet-ups, or exclusive brand events can strengthen community bonds and make members feel valued.

12. Ensure easy accessibility

Your community should be easy to find and join. If it’s on your website, use clear signage from your homepage, product pages, or checkout process to highlight its existence and benefits.

13. Run cross-promotion campaigns

Utilize branded hashtags or launch challenges that bridge different platforms. This cross-channel strategy maintains consistent brand messaging and introduces your community to a broader audience, attracting potential new members.
