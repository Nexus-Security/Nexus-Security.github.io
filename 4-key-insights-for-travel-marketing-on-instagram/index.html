4 Key Insights for Travel Marketing on Instagram

Nov 22, 2022
8 minute read

The travel sector is expanding rapidly, establishing itself as one of the largest and most fiercely competitive industries globally. Modern consumers are increasingly allocating their budgets towards travel, with a significant 34% of millennials planning to spend upwards of $5,000 on their upcoming vacations, as revealed by Business Insider. To differentiate themselves in this crowded market, travel industry stakeholders must stay ahead of the curve. one podcast by Facebook emphasizes the considerable influence of Instagram in this domain, reporting that 70% of travel enthusiasts utilize the platform to share their travel itineraries, 67% seek inspiration for new adventures, and 61% rely on it to discover activities during their trips. Recognizing this trend, brands such as National Geographic have amassed a staggering following of over 128 million users on Instagram, solidifying its position as the most followed brand account on the platform. Whether you represent a multinational corporation, an airline, a car rental agency, or even a travel blog, Instagram presents an unparalleled opportunity to connect with your target demographic, enhance brand visibility, foster loyalty and trust, and ultimately, drive revenue growth.

Image Source In essence, Instagram has become an indispensable marketing instrument for visual storytelling, captivating the attention of travelers worldwide. To elevate your travel marketing game on Instagram, consider these four invaluable lessons gleaned from leading players in the travel industry:

  1. A visually consistent feed is a magnet for followers
  2. Social proof is the cornerstone of loyalty
  3. In-app shopping is experiencing a surge in popularity
  4. Exceptional customer service is non-negotiable

Lesson #1: A Harmonious Feed Attracts More Followers

Acquiring new customers is a primary objective for virtually every travel company. The size of your Instagram following directly correlates with your pool of potential customers. To distinguish yourself from competitors and attract a larger following, prioritize the visual appeal of your Instagram feed, making it captivating and scroll-stopping, as highlighted by 65% of people are visual learners. Furthermore, a significant 72% of Millennials and Gen X travelers share their travel moments on social media. Consequently, it’s hardly surprising that 67% of individuals consider the “Instagrammability” of a destination as a crucial factor in their travel decisions, as per a study conducted by Expedia. In fact, 40.1% of young travelers prioritize holiday destinations based on their potential for stunning Instagram content. Showcasing captivating destinations is paramount for inspiring wanderlust in potential travelers. STA Travel exemplifies this by leveraging user-generated photos to curate a visually stunning and cohesive Instagram feed.

Lesson #2: Social Proof Cultivates Loyalty and Trust

In an age of overwhelming choices, brand loyalty and trust are the differentiating factors that set you apart from the competition vying for your target audience’s attention. Notably, travelers often rely on authentic social proof for inspiration and reassurance during the travel planning phase. Travelers are astute at identifying trust signals, relying on travel vlogs, customer reviews, and personal recommendations before committing to a booking. A staggering 52% of social media users find travel inspiration directly from their friends’ photos. This underscores the importance of incorporating social proof into your Instagram strategy, and fortunately, numerous avenues exist for achieving this. Outdoor Research, a company specializing in technical apparel and gear for outdoor enthusiasts, leverages collaborations with niche influencers to cultivate social proof, yielding impressive results. According to a case study from Talkable, implementing a referral program has propelled Outdoor Research to achieve a remarkable 600% return on investment and a 17% advocacy rate. Lesson #3: In-App Shopping is Gaining Momentum

The popularity of Instagram shopping is undeniable. Instagram Business reports that a significant 60% of Instagrammers discover new products or services on the platform, with a staggering 130 million accounts engaging with shopping posts monthly to gather product information. Capitalizing on the growing user inclination towards in-app purchases, Instagram has introduced an array of business-centric features. These include product tags and shoppable stickers, strategically placed “Book” and “Reserve” action buttons, and clickable links within Instagram Stories, all aimed at enhancing the customer experience. For travel companies, this signifies a readily available customer base primed for booking travel experiences directly through Instagram. Embracing in-app selling is crucial, and Ryanair’s strategic approach exemplifies this concept. Lesson #4: Superior Customer Service is Imperative

Customer service expectations have undergone a paradigm shift in recent years. The days of relying solely on traveler hotlines are long gone. Today’s discerning customers leverage social media platforms, particularly Instagram, to share their experiences and seek resolution for their concerns. A recent Sprout Social report revealed that 46% of customers have utilized social media to connect with brands, with 55% expecting a timely resolution or response via these channels. Travel industry players must be prepared to navigate a wave of feedback, both positive and negative, on Instagram. Take Skyscanner’s experience as a case in point. Customers frequently utilize the comment section to voice their pain points, necessitating swift responses from the company to address concerns, manage expectations, and reassure potential travelers of their worth.

Consequently, providing exceptional customer support on Instagram is no longer optional but essential. Notably, 73% of customers affirm that friendly service plays a pivotal role in fostering brand affinity. Actively listening to your customers and addressing their inquiries allows you to outmaneuver competitors and cultivate a loyal customer base. Key Takeaway: Elevating customer satisfaction and providing compelling reasons for choosing your company over competitors hinges on delivering exceptional customer support on Instagram. Diligently monitor comments, respond to direct messages promptly, and track brand mentions to ensure swift resolution of customer inquiries.

Position Your Travel Instagram for Success

Modern consumers have an insatiable appetite for travel, frequently turning to Instagram for destination discovery, vacation inspiration, and booking travel experiences. With the travel industry being one of the most profitable Instagram niches, it’s no surprise that companies are eager to harness the power of Instagram marketing.
