The Singapore International Festival of Music is ready for its October shows

Oct 01, 2022
10 minute read

The Singapore International Festival of Music (SIFOM), Singapore’s first classical music festival, returns for its second year. Centered around the theme “Myths and Legends,” the festival showcases classical music inspired by folklore and fairy tales. From October 13th to 30th, the festival offers free public programs, masterclasses, and ticketed events including orchestral performances, musical theater, and vocal concerts.

The 2016 SIFOM lineup features the festival’s own SIFOM Orchestra and Ensemble, Ding Yi Music Company (showcasing Singapore’s finest young musicians), and internationally-renowned guests from the Chamber Orchestra of Europe (COE), a distinguished chamber orchestra.

Collaborating with the Arts House and OperaViva, SIFOM seeks to establish Singapore as a global classical music destination and attract new audiences. “Singapore has an undeniably international brand. By positioning Singapore as the region’s classical music hub, SIFOM aims to further solidify Singapore’s global standing as a major cultural center,” remarked Grammy-nominated Singaporean conductor Darrell Ang, SIFOM’s Artistic Director.

The festival commences with a symphonic concert featuring the resident SIFOM Orchestra alongside members of the COE, led by Maestro Ang. COE will also present a performance based on French composer Maurice Ravel’s orchestration of tales from Mother Goose, including Sleeping Beauty, Tom Thumb, The Princess of the Pagodas, Beauty and the Beast, and The Fairy Garden.

Two rising opera stars from the Musical Olympus Foundation will take the stage on October 13th and 15th, performing a selection of their favorite pieces. Chinese bass-baritone Li Ao, winner of Placido Domingo’s Operalia competition, will portray the devil Mephistopheles in a renowned aria from Gounod’s opera Faust, where he advises a woman to remain chaste until marriage. Vasilisa Berzhanskaya, from Moscow’s Bolshoi Theatre, will perform Cinderella’s concluding aria from Rossini’s opera based on the fairytale, in which the now-married Cinderella seeks forgiveness for her mistreatment by her wicked stepfamily.

Aleksey Semenenko, an exceptional violinist who recently earned the silver medal at the Queen Elisabeth Competition, promises an electrifying performance featuring music from Sarasate’s Carmen Fantasy. His evening concert on October 13th will commence with a pre-concert recital showcasing music inspired by legends, followed by Russian composer Nikolai Rimsky-Korsakov’s symphonic adaptation of the adventures of Sindbad the Sailor and the 1001 Arabian Nights.

Inspired by a traditional Malay poem called Syair Bidasari, the story of Ikan Girl highlights the victory of good over evil. The performance blends elements of Malay dance, Indian bharatanatyam, and contemporary dance styles with original music composed by Syafiqah Adha Sallehin, Artist-in-Residence at SIFOM 2016. Renowned percussionist Ramu Thiruyanam will make a special appearance during the live performance by classical and traditional musicians.

On October 13th, the Hamburg baroque orchestra Elbipolis will lead an experimental concert series, featuring the music of Tim Exile and Brezel Göring, fusing elements of a traditional concert with a club atmosphere.

On October 19th, enjoy a performance entitled “New Music in a Mythic Landscape,” showcasing original compositions by SIFOM Young Composers, inspired by the scenery and landmarks of Singapore.

“Classical music shouldn’t be confined to concert halls. By bringing it to local communities, we hope to make classical music accessible to everyone and cultivate a new generation of enthusiasts,” said Ang.

As part of its Outreach Programs, SIFOM musicians will perform excerpts from the main programs, such as Ravel’s Mother Goose Suite, in a variety of community spaces including parks, malls, libraries, schools, and heartland areas.

Additionally, SIFOM will collaborate with Sentire Singapore on a program for children with special needs. Designed to encourage self-expression and artistic development, the program will culminate in a live performance by some of the students at The Arts House on October 19th.
