Email subject line best practices

Here are some general email subject line best practices to incorporate into your email marketing endeavors.

  • Put your subject lines to the test. Craft 3-5 subject lines per email and select the most promising one, or employ A/B testing to identify the winners. Remember that after the iOS 15 update, relying solely on open rates to determine winners might not be the most effective approach.
  • Brevity is key. Aim for 30 to 50 characters. According to a MailChimp study, emails with subject lines containing 50 characters or less exhibit 12% higher open rates and 75% higher click-through rates compared to their counterparts.
  • Steer clear of spam traps. Refrain from using unusual spacing, excessive punctuation or capitalization, or special fonts. Also, avoid spam trigger words such as “earn extra cash,” “make $,” “get out of debt,” “click here,” “100% free,” “will not believe your eyes,” and other clickbait phrases.
  • Personalize whenever feasible: Go beyond simply including their name; incorporate information relevant to their location, past purchases, interests, and more. You can achieve this using email automation with dynamic tokens.
  • Utilize preheader text. Think of this as your subject line’s subtitle, providing an opportunity to add more detail and enhance the email’s allure. Relocate the “view in browser” links and other extraneous elements to the email’s bottom to maximize the preview field.

How to write great email subject lines

  • Capture their attention instantly. Employ intriguing words, a compelling statistic, a relatable phrase, or highlight the value proposition.
  • Infuse them with personality. A touch of alliteration can work wonders! As can rhyming, song lyrics, and emojis â€“ those tiny communication marvels of the digital age.
  • Adopt a conversational tone. Write as you would speak. Even if your brand maintains a more formal voice, a conversational style enhances readability and fosters a sense of friendliness. For instance, opt for contractions like “you’re” instead of “you are.”
  • Incorporate a call to action. Including a call to action phrase in your email subject line is always a good practice. This clarifies the email’s purpose and resonates with readers.
  • Pose a question: Engaging your readers with a question instead of a standard statement instantly piques their interest. Questions initiate a direct dialogue with users, making them more inclined to open the email.
    • Having a rough day?
    • Are you making these X mistakes?
    • Ready to save 50%?
  • Draw inspiration from your own inbox. If you come across impressive subject lines that you believe could work for your business, don’t hesitate to borrow them! Adapt them to your liking and align them with your brand voice.
  • Highlight scarcity: Deep within us lies an inherent fear of being left behind, of missing out. This herd mentality, once a survival instinct, now presents an opportunity to inject urgency into our subject lines.
    • Pay X for Y (48-hour exclusive offer!)
    • Ending today! 36-hour outlet sale. Last chanceâ€¦
    • 5 hours only!
  • Leverage brackets and parentheses: These punctuation marks effectively highlight important information without resorting to capitalization, and they help organize large amounts of information.
  • Use “you/your.” While name-calling is a no-go, using “you” and “your” wording remains a best practice, fostering a direct and comfortable connection with readers.
  • Be specific. Even when incorporating an element of mystery into your subject line, ensure it provides readers with a general idea of what awaits them.
