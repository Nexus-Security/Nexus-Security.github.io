Small business ideas from home

Remote work is incredibly popular for good reason. Why commute when you can make money from the comfort of your own home? Say goodbye to traffic jams, pricey lunches, and unavoidable coworkers. Here are some fantastic small business ideas you can launch from home:

1. Childcare business

With many families adopting remote work, daycare providers are in high demand. If you’re a stay-at-home parent, you can offer daycare services from home and care for your children simultaneously. Average yearly salary: $29,000

2. Virtual fitness trainer

Gyms experienced a decline in memberships during and after the pandemic as people discovered the convenience of home workouts. The popularity of online fitness trainers skyrocketed, creating a demand for diverse exercise experiences. This means online fitness training, whether on YouTube, Instagram, or platforms like MindBody, is a thriving business opportunity. Trainers earn between $30,000 – $70,000 per year, but your income as an online trainer depends on your pricing model.

3. Web developer

Becoming a web developer requires training and initial education costs. However, numerous bootcamps can help you gain the necessary skills quickly. One of the biggest advantages of web development is the wide range of specializations available. You can focus on marketing websites, e-commerce platforms, blogs, or any other online presence. If coding isn’t your forte, consider starting a small business that manages freelance web developers. It’s a highly sought-after service! Web developers earn a respectable salary, starting around $60,000 per year and reaching up to $150,000 depending on expertise and location.

4. Executive assistant

The rapid growth of the tech industry has led to a surge in executives who need assistance with administrative tasks. As an executive assistant, you can work remotely for one or multiple companies, offering part-time support and building a thriving small business. Executive assistants can earn around $60,000 or more per year.

5. Nonprofit Work

Starting a small business is even more rewarding when you know you’re making a positive impact. While starting a nonprofit has its challenges, it can be done remotely, particularly if you’re running it solo. Choose a cause you’re passionate about and take the plunge!

Unique small business ideas

When we talk about “good” small businesses, we mean those that offer enduring and stable services that address consistent needs. For example, people will always need to eat, so a food-related business can be a safe bet. Don’t worry; these unique small business ideas don’t involve plucking chickens.

6. Translator

The increasing globalization of our world has fueled the demand for translation services. This includes translating emails, documents, and other content, as well as providing in-person interpretation services for groups or business leaders during international trips. Keep in mind that translation requires not only conversational fluency but also a strong grasp of grammar and sentence structure.

7. Tutor

If you’re an adult who has completed high school or college, you likely have knowledge you can share with students. Consider tutoring if you enjoy teaching. You can work with multiple students on various subjects, like SAT prep or remedial Spanish. Tutoring offers flexibility in terms of time commitment and is in high demand, even for remote services.

8. Nanny

Families continue to grow, creating a steady need for nannies. If you enjoy spending time with children, using your imagination, and packing snacks, starting a nanny business might be a good fit. Depending on your availability and the family’s needs, nanny positions can range from part-time to full-time and may include benefits like room and board.

9. IT consulting

Every small business needs IT support, making this a consistently in-demand service. If you’re tech-savvy and know how to troubleshoot IT issues, create a website and start offering your expertise. IT professionals command excellent rates, whether they charge per hour or per project.

Image source

10. Online advertiser

Digital marketing is an ever-evolving field, and businesses need to invest in online strategies to thrive. However, many small business owners wear multiple hats and lack the time to stay updated on the latest digital marketing trends. Consider offering online advertising services to these businesses and help them navigate the complex world of online marketing. Small profitable business ideas

Starting a business is about more than passion; it’s also about generating income. These small business ideas have the potential to be highly profitable, ensuring a steady stream of revenue.

11. Handyman

Handymen are invaluable for their ability to tackle various home repair and maintenance tasks. If you enjoy working with your hands and figuring out how to fix things, starting a handyman business could be a good fit. Handyman rates vary depending on the type and duration of the job. Related: Home Services Advertising Benchmarks

12. Event planner

Event planning combines creativity with practicality. People will always celebrate milestones like birthdays, weddings, and anniversaries, creating a steady demand for event planners. If you’re highly organized, detail-oriented, and skilled at managing people, consider turning event planning into a business.

Image source

13. Transcriptionist

With the increasing popularity of video content, the demand for transcription services is higher than ever. There are various types of transcription, including casual transcription for marketing videos and specialized transcription for legal or medical purposes. Specialized transcription often requires specific certifications, which are relatively affordable to obtain. Transcriptionists earn an average of $52,000 per year.

14. Movers

The real estate market has experienced significant fluctuations in recent years, leading to an increase in people moving. Starting a moving business can be a lucrative opportunity to capitalize on this trend, as people are always looking for reliable movers. Movers typically charge per hour or per job, particularly for multi-day or long-distance moves.

Image source

15. Antiquities renovation

This niche business caters to those who appreciate historical preservation. If you have a knack for restoring and reinstalling vintage fixtures, such as cabinet pulls, bricks, or old window panes, there’s a market for your skills, particularly in areas with historic homes. 16. Short-term rental manager

The rise of platforms like Airbnb and VRBO has created a demand for short-term rental managers. Many property owners struggle to handle the day-to-day operations of managing their rentals, including cleaning, communication, and guest services. Consider offering your services as a short-term rental manager to help hosts maximize their earnings and provide a seamless experience for guests. Rental manager salaries depend on location and are generally higher in urban areas.

Easy small business ideas for teens or adults

Looking for a side hustle that doesn’t require too much effort? These easy-to-start small businesses are a great way to earn extra income without overwhelming yourself.

17. Pet care

Pet care can be a rewarding and relatively easy business to start. You can offer services like dog walking, cat sitting, chicken checks, horse feeding, and more. You have the flexibility to choose the jobs and hours that work for you, and you might even get to enjoy house sitting as a bonus. Pet sitters typically earn between $10-$25 per hour, depending on the number of pets and location.

Image source

18. Proofreading/quality assurance

If you’re detail-oriented and have excellent writing skills, consider becoming a proofreader or quality assurance specialist. These professions are always in high demand, as businesses and individuals need help ensuring the accuracy and quality of their written content. You can find freelance work on platforms like UpWork while you build your portfolio. Proofreaders and QA specialists can earn up to $80,000 per year.

19. Notary

Becoming a notary public is a relatively straightforward process that can open up opportunities to offer your services to individuals, businesses, and even courthouses. Once you’re certified, you can set your hours and choose the projects you want to take on. Notary salaries range from $20,000-$52,000 per year, but the flexibility and high demand make it an attractive option.

20. Personal shopper

Personal shoppers curate and purchase items for clients who don’t have the time or desire to shop for themselves. With the growth of e-commerce, much of this work can be done remotely. Personal shoppers earn an average of $60,000 per year.

Creative small business ideas for women

If you’re looking to tap into your creative side and turn it into a business, here are a few ideas to inspire you.

21. Landscaper

Landscaping is a rewarding and creative profession for those with a passion for gardening and transforming outdoor spaces. As a landscaper, you can create beautiful gardens, maintain lawns, and enhance properties with your skills. Landscapers can charge $10-25/hour or price their services per project.

22. Gift kit creator

Curated gift boxes filled with personalized items like snacks, wine, gadgets, and other goodies have become increasingly popular. Starting a gift kit business allows you to express your creativity by sourcing unique items and assembling themed boxes for various occasions. With drop-shipping options readily available, you can minimize upfront costs and get started quickly.

Image source

23. Travel planner

Travel planning is an excellent option for those with a passion for travel and exploring new destinations. As a travel planner, you can specialize in specific regions, age groups, or travel styles, creating custom itineraries and helping clients make the most of their trips. You’ll also enjoy perks like discounted hotel rates and exclusive access to travel industry insights. Travel agents typically earn between $35,000-$67,000 per year.

24. Influencer

While some may not view it as a traditional business, becoming an influencer requires creativity, marketing skills, and the ability to connect with a target audience. There are numerous courses available to help aspiring influencers navigate the world of social media and build a following. Influencer income can vary greatly depending on factors like audience size, engagement rates, and brand partnerships. The rise of influencers highlights the power of social media marketing for any business.

25. Thrifting reseller

Thrift flipping has evolved into a popular online business. If you have an eye for finding hidden gems at thrift stores and enjoy giving old items a new lease on life, consider starting a thrifting reseller business. You can create your own brand and sell your curated finds on platforms like eBay.

How do you find the best business to start?

The most successful businesses solve problems for specific groups of people. When choosing a small business idea, focus on identifying a niche where your skills and expertise can truly shine.
