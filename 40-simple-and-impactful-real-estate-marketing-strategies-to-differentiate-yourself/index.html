40 Simple and Impactful Real Estate Marketing Strategies to Differentiate Yourself

Apr 23, 2023
16 minute read

Although real estate agents remain crucial in the process of purchasing a home, prospective buyers are increasingly conducting preliminary research online before seeking professional guidance. A study conducted by the National Association of Realtors revealed that 92% of those looking to buy a home utilize the internet to initiate their search, highlighting the essential need for real estate agents to establish a strong online presence. A lack of online activity, engagement, and networking translates to missed opportunities.

Presented below are over 40 effective real estate marketing strategies for those aiming to excel in the competitive real estate market.

Real Estate Marketing Strategies for Your Website

In today’s digital landscape, a website is not merely a marketing strategy but an absolute necessity. Consumers anticipate businesses to have an online presence, and a poorly designed website can be more detrimental than having no website at all. Below are some strategies to ensure your website effectively promotes your real estate business 24/7.

1. Craft an Impressive Homepage

Did you know that 75% of individuals acknowledge forming an opinion about a business’s credibility based on the design of its website? Initial impressions are paramount, making it imperative for your homepage to be in excellent condition. This translates to incorporating captivating visuals, a noticeable call to action, and a user-friendly menu.

2. Develop Exceptional Property Listings

Today‚Äôs consumers prefer to conduct extensive online research independently when making significant purchases, and this behavior extends to buying a home. Having only rented myself, when searching for properties, I utilize Google Maps to locate the address, explore the neighborhood using Street View, identify nearby businesses within walking distance, browse through property photos, and, if available, take a virtual tour of the property. Your property pages and listings should facilitate seamless access to all this information‚Äîaccompanied by comprehensive descriptions, high-quality photos, virtual tours, and convenient links to Google Maps and Google Earth. Highlight nearby attractions and their respective distances (for instance, mentioning the bus stop that‚Äôs a brief five-minute walk away, or the Starbucks conveniently located at the end of the block).

3. Engage a Professional Photographer

It is absolutely crucial to have stunning photos showcasing your properties. The appropriate time of day or camera angle can significantly impact the outcome. Employ the services of a professional photographer (preferably someone with experience in photographing homes and architecture), or, if you possess advanced equipment and confidence in your abilities, consider taking the photos yourself. However, keep in mind that this is one of those situations where it is worthwhile to engage professionals.

4. Utilize Lead Magnets

The secret to lead generation lies in providing your audience with something valuable enough in exchange for their contact information. Contemplate creating a lead magnet guide such as ‚ÄúX Common Mistakes First-Time Homebuyers Make‚Äù or ‚ÄúX Desirable Features to Seek In a New Home.‚Äù things new homeowners should consider before buying‚Äù or ‚ÄúX most valuable features of a new home.‚Äù Offering complimentary ebooks in exchange for an email address (and zip code, in the context of real estate) is a highly effective method for capturing high-quality leads.

5. Prioritize User Experience

The presence of exceptional property pages will be rendered insignificant if your website is challenging to navigate. Your visitors should have a positive experience on your website, so dedicate time to enhance your user experience design skills and understanding of information architecture.

An aesthetically pleasing website serves no purpose if it remains undiscovered! SEO encompasses a set of practices that work in unison to elevate your site’s ranking in search engine results. This includes:

  • On-page SEO: Incorporating your targeted keywords for each page strategically within the page’s title, meta descriptions, image file names and alt text, and organically within the body content.

Real Estate Marketing Strategies for Social Media

Social media marketing is a crucial component of your real estate business. Here are some strategies for leveraging these platforms to effectively market your real estate business.

7. Establish a Strong Social Media Presence

The most prevalent social media platforms for real estate marketing are Facebook, LinkedIn, and Instagram. When creating your accounts, ensure you complete all available fields to maximize your discoverability by relevant users.

8. Utilize Relevant Hashtags

9. Provide Valuable Content

Social media offers numerous opportunities for real estate businesses to cultivate an audience, interact with followers, and nurture relationships. Here are a few real estate social media marketing ideas:

  • Leverage YouTube, Facebook, or Instagram Live to conduct virtual home tours and host Q&A sessions.
  • Share concise and practical real estate tips.
  • Disseminate news relevant to your community and niche.
  • Promote your latest property listings.
  • Participate in relevant Facebook and LinkedIn groups.

10. Incorporate Social Share Buttons on Property Pages

Homebuyers are often eager to share their favorite housing options with friends and family, so simplify the process of emailing and online sharing of properties by incorporating social sharing buttons.

Despite having the lowest organic reach among social media platforms, Facebook’s targeting capabilities for paid advertising remain unparalleled. Pinterest advertising and marketing continue to be effective for certain industries‚Äîreal estate being one of them. Boards provide an excellent platform for showcasing images and information for specific property listings. Consider crafting a Pinterest board dedicated to a single property, which, in addition to property photos, highlights the features and advantages of the surrounding area.

16. Contribute as a Columnist in Local Magazines

Explore opportunities to write a regular column or contribute articles to local publications. Avoid being overly promotional; today‚Äôs consumers are averse to aggressive sales tactics. Instead, share valuable insights and perspectives. For instance, write about how rising home prices in the market reflect the town’s economic prosperity, or highlight factors contributing to the region’s growing popularity (such as a revitalized downtown area or an improved school system).

17. Organize Free Seminars for Homebuyers

Position yourself as a knowledgeable resource within the community by conducting brief seminars. Consider offering an introductory seminar covering the fundamentals of purchasing a home and securing a mortgage. Remember, modern marketing emphasizes an inbound approach, and this principle extends beyond the online realm. Users appreciate access to free, valuable information before investing their time and money in your services. A homebuying seminar serves as the offline equivalent of a webinar. While it requires time and effort, attendees will leave with a positive impression and an established relationship with you. This relationship will prove invaluable when they are ready to embark on their home-buying journey.

18. Collaborate with Local Businesses

Forging partnerships with other businesses in your local area is crucial for your real estate marketing endeavors. Explore collaborations such as providing local eateries with new coffee mugs featuring your logo, or securing advertising space on their paper placemat settings. Alternatively, consider partnering with local moving companies to exchange referrals.

Real Estate Email Marketing Strategies

Email marketing has consistently ranked among the most effective marketing strategies across industries and stages of the sales funnel. Here are some real estate marketing strategies utilizing email.

19. Create a Newsletter

Gather email sign-ups through your website and events, and distribute a helpful newsletter featuring new listings, details about upcoming seminars you’re hosting, local events, helpful tips, and more. Remember to segment your email lists based on different communities to deliver highly relevant content.

Employing a creative call to action for newsletter sign-ups can be beneficial.

20. Implement an Email Nurture Campaign

Nurture campaigns can be likened to Hansel and Gretel’s breadcrumb trail, except instead of a gingerbread house awaiting at the end, clients discover their ideal home (hopefully without encountering an evil witch). Guide your clients with a trail of metaphorical breadcrumbs by personalizing your interactions based on their previous actions. For instance, if they attended an open house you hosted, send them an email showcasing other available homes in the vicinity. If they participated in your first-time homebuyer seminar, send them your ebook titled ‚Äú10 Things Every New Homebuyer Should Know.‚Äù Deliver content that empowers your clients to take the appropriate next step, tailored to their current stage in the home-buying process.

21. Send Greeting Emails

Here‚Äôs a simple yet effective email marketing tip: send personalized emails to clients during holidays or on their birthdays or anniversaries. A small gesture can have a significant impact, and satisfied clients are more inclined to recommend your services to others. Steer clear of generic email templates! Traditional Real Estate Marketing Strategies

Despite the prominence of digital marketing, it’s essential not to overlook the importance of the physical world! Here are some effective offline real estate marketing strategies that remain as relevant as ever.

22. Branded Merchandise

Leveraging business branding is beneficial‚Äîconsider items like pens, drink koozies, and other popular giveaways. Distribute branded merchandise at local festivals and events to increase brand awareness.

23. Utilize Call Tracking

Call tracking is an indispensable tool for paid search campaigns in the real estate industry. Most individuals rely on phone calls when searching for a realtor or scheduling an appointment to view an apartment, condo, or house. If these calls originate from your PPC ads, you need a system to track which specific ads and keywords are driving those calls.

24. Design a Striking Business Card

Invest in visually appealing business cards and distribute them generously. Don’t forget to incorporate your contact information into your email signature as well!

Canva offers a wide selection of free real estate business card templates to choose from.

25. Traditional Mailers

While the internet dominates the marketing landscape, traditional advertising methods still hold value, especially when targeting specific audiences. Consider sending postcards to qualified leads or potential buyers residing in the zip codes where you actively operate.

26. Provide a Local Care Package After Closing

Your relationship with a client shouldn’t end immediately after the sale. It’s essential to maintain a positive impression so they remember you and potentially recommend you to friends, family, or acquaintances considering a move in the future. Consider sending a local care package to clients after closing, including items like movie or theater tickets for local venues, gift cards to local restaurants, and other thoughtful gestures.

Additional Real Estate Marketing Strategies

Here are more imaginative and practical strategies to help your real estate business flourish.

31. Establish a Presence on Zillow

In the realm of real estate marketing, Zillow reigns supreme, much like Yelp does for other industries. Maintaining a presence on Zillow is crucial for visibility‚Äîignoring its significance is akin to disregarding the sun; its impact will be felt regardless! Zillow offers advertising opportunities for agents on their platform. While these can be costly, considering Zillow’s dominant role in the homebuying process, it is likely a worthwhile investment. After all, platforms like Zillow command a significant 48% of all website traffic related to real estate searches. Moreover, Zillow enables users to review real estate agents, so prioritizing excellent service and accumulating positive ratings is vital, as a strong star rating can considerably boost your leads.

32. Request Testimonials from Past Clients

Testimonials serve as powerful trust signals. Demonstrating that real individuals endorse your services holds immense weight with potential clients. After successfully assisting a homebuyer, reach out to request a testimonial. If feasible, obtain their photo as well. Maximize the impact of these testimonials by strategically featuring them on your website and periodically sharing them on social media.

33. Host Webinars

If you are comfortable with public speaking, hosting a webinar can effectively attract attention to your business. Consider a webinar titled ‚Äú12 Little-Known Factors to Consider In A New Home,‚Äù incorporating a Q&A session at the end to address participant inquiries. You can also repurpose webinars as YouTube videos (find more real estate video marketing ideas here), providing valuable video content that remains permanently accessible on your website! For additional guidance, explore our tips for creating exceptional webinars.

34. Monitor the Competition

Stay informed about the strategies employed by other realtors in your area. What do their websites look like? How actively do they engage on social media? 35. Offer Virtual Tours

Your clients’ time is valuable, and they appreciate the opportunity to gather as much information as possible about a property before visiting in person. Virtual tours are an excellent way to provide prospective buyers with a comprehensive and accurate preview of the property. Most platforms, including Zillow, offer user-friendly tools for creating virtual tours.

36. Leverage Shared Scheduling Apps

Utilize a shared scheduling app to simplify the process of coordinating property showings with potential buyers. Apps like Doodle can significantly reduce the back-and-forth communication often associated with scheduling.

38. Maintain Communication

Nurture ongoing relationships with past clients, even months or years later, to foster goodwill and potential referrals. Sending anniversary cards, holiday cards, and other thoughtful gestures helps keep you top-of-mind. When someone they know is ready to buy a home, your name will be fresh in their memory.

39. Establish a Referral Program

Incentivize past clients to refer their friends and family by implementing a structured referral program.

40. Consider Niche Specialization

If you face intense real estate market competition in your area, consider differentiating yourself through niche marketing. Position yourself as the preferred real estate agent for specific demographics, such as dog owners, families with children, or divorced individuals. Establishing expertise within a particular niche can make you memorable, especially in areas with high realtor saturation.
